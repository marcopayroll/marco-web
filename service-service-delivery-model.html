SERVICES · SERVICE DELIVERY MODEL

How Marco Structures
EOR service delivery

EOR is not just about placing an employee under a contract. The delivery model defines who is involved, who the legal employer is, how work visa support is handled, how payroll and benefits are managed, and how communication flows between the client, Marco, local partners, and the employee.

WHY IT MATTERS

The HR team you do not have to hire locally.

EOR arrangements involve more than one relationship. There may be a client, Marco, an employee, and a local service delivery partner. The same service name can be understood differently depending on the actual structure in place.

A regulator may look at the arrangement and assess who employs the person, who provides the local employment setup, and whether it looks like EOR, contract staffing, or another local model. The delivery structure determines how the employment relationship, work visa support, payroll, benefits, and HR operations are actually carried out.

Legal employer clarity

Who signs the employment contract and holds the employer obligations matters to regulators and employees alike.

Work visa and EP support

Immigration arrangements are tied directly to the employment structure. The wrong model can invalidate a visa application or renewal.

Payroll and benefit accountability

Who processes payroll, who makes payment, and who registers statutory benefits must be clearly defined in advance.

Model confusion carries real risk

EOR and contract staffing can look similar from the outside but carry different legal treatment depending on the country and regulator.

WHO IS INVOLVED

Every EOR setup has clear parties.

An EOR arrangement connects multiple parties. Each one has a defined role. Clarity on who does what is the foundation of compliant delivery.

Client

The company that needs support hiring or supporting an employee in a market. The client retains operational direction over the employee's work.

1

Marco

The service provider coordinating the EOR arrangement, documents, process, and communication between all parties throughout the engagement.

2

Employee

The individual being supported under the EOR arrangement. The employee holds an employment contract and receives payroll, benefits, and HR support through the structure.

3

Local Service Delivery Partner

In some markets, a local partner may be involved where local delivery support is required. Their role, responsibilities, and relationship to Marco must be clearly documented.

4
EOR SCOPE

What must be clear in an EOR arrangement.

Five elements must be defined and documented before EOR delivery begins. These define the arrangement, the obligations, and the accountability.

01

Legal Employer

Who acts as the legal employer under the arrangement. This party holds employer obligations and accountability in the host country.

02

Legal Employee Status

The employee's legal status in the host country, including work visa or EP support where the employee requires it to work legally.

03

Employment Contract

The employment contract structure: which party is named, what jurisdiction applies, and whether a local contract or assignment letter is used.

04

Payroll and Benefits

How salary is processed and paid, who handles statutory benefit contributions, and how benefits are administered for the employee.

05

HR Operations Support

How HR support is delivered to the employee day to day: who communicates with the employee, who handles time-off, expenses, and HR queries.

LOCAL DELIVERY

When local delivery support is involved.

In some EOR arrangements, a local service delivery partner may be involved. This is typically where local employment support, work visa matters, or country-specific service delivery requires a licensed local entity to be part of the structure.

The local partner's role must be clearly documented. This includes their relationship to Marco, their specific responsibilities in the arrangement, and how they interact with the client and employee.

The involvement of a local partner must be disclosed and agreed upon before delivery begins. The structure of that relationship has direct implications for how the employment arrangement is viewed by local regulators.

WHAT MUST BE DOCUMENTED

Local partner role and scope

The specific services the local partner provides, the basis on which they engage with Marco, and what they are and are not responsible for in the arrangement.

WHAT MUST BE DOCUMENTED

Legal and contractual basis

Formal agreements between Marco and the local partner must be in place before delivery begins, covering service scope, liability, and data handling obligations.

WHAT MUST BE DOCUMENTED

Communication flow

Who communicates with the employee, who handles HR queries, and who the employee contacts for support must be defined clearly so there is no ambiguity in the relationship.

NOT A UNIVERSAL MODEL

Local partners are market-specific

Whether a local service delivery partner is involved depends on the country, the nature of the engagement, and the local regulatory environment. This is not a fixed part of every EOR arrangement.

MODEL CLARITY

EOR and contract staffing are not the same.

The two arrangements can look similar from the outside but may carry different legal treatment, obligations, and regulatory classification depending on the country.

EOR

Employer of Record

Under an EOR arrangement, a service provider acts as the legal employer on behalf of the client. The EOR holds the employment contract, processes payroll and benefits, handles HR operations, and provides work visa support where needed. The client directs the employee's work but is not the employing entity.

CONTRACT STAFFING

Contract Staffing

Under a contract staffing arrangement, a staffing agency supplies labour to a client on a contract basis. The agency may or may not hold employer obligations depending on the local model. In some jurisdictions, this structure is treated very differently from EOR by regulators, particularly around immigration and employment law.

WHY THIS DISTINCTION MATTERS:

In some countries, regulators may assess an arrangement and view it as contract staffing even if it was set up as EOR. The delivery structure, the parties involved, and the documented relationships determine how the arrangement is classified in practice.

DELIVERY MODEL

Direct and indirect EOR need clear explanation.

EOR delivery can be structured in more than one way. The right model depends on the country, local requirements, the employee's situation, and the service scope.

DIRECT EOR

Direct delivery structure

A structure where the EOR arrangement is delivered more directly through the provider's own setup. The service provider's entity takes on the employer role and manages delivery without a separate local intermediary in the employment chain.

INDIRECT EOR

Partner-supported delivery structure

A structure where another local party or partner is involved in the delivery arrangement. The local partner may act as the employing entity or provide specific local services within the arrangement. This structure must be clearly defined and documented for all parties.

The correct model depends on the country, local requirements, the employee's situation, and the service scope.

Marco advises on the appropriate delivery structure before any engagement begins so all parties understand the arrangement and its implications.

RELATED MODELS

Different markets use different employment models.

EOR is not the only employment model used across markets. These related structures are referenced in different countries and may be relevant for comparison or classification purposes.

EA

Employment Agency

A local employment agency or employment arrangement concept. The EA structure and its legal treatment varies by country and may carry different obligations compared to EOR.

PAYROLLING

Payrolling

A model in which a service provider takes an employee already selected by the client onto its payroll. The Netherlands government formally defines payrolling as a distinct model from staffing and direct employment.

Netherlands government definition available

AUG

Ar­beit­neh­mer­u­ber­las­sung

A regulated employment leasing concept under German law. AUG governs how a company may legally supply employees to a third party on a temporary basis, with specific licensing and duration requirements.

German government definition available

PORTAGE

Salary Portage

A salary portage model used in France in which an independent professional works through a portage company that handles their employment formalities. Formally defined under French labour law.

French government definition available

These models are not interchangeable. Each one may carry different legal meaning, responsibility, and compliance treatment depending on the country. Marco's service delivery model clarifies which structure is being used before delivery begins.

BEFORE DELIVERY

The key questions to confirm first

These questions should be answered and documented before any EOR engagement begins. They define the structure, the parties, and the obligations for everyone involved.

Who is the legal employer?

Who handles work visa or EP support for the employee?

Who provides HR operations support to the employee?

Is a local service delivery partner involved, and if so, what is their documented role?

What employment contract applies and which party is involved?

Who processes payroll and benefits?

Who communicates with the employee and manages day-to-day contact?

Is the arrangement EOR, contract staffing, or another local employment model?
