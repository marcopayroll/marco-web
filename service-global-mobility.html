WHAT IS GLOBAL MOBILITY

Your people, where the business needs them

Global mobility is the practice of moving employees across borders for work assignments, whether short-term projects, long-term relocations, or permanent transfers. It involves visa and immigration, employment contracts, payroll structuring, tax compliance, and ongoing HR support in both the home and host country.

Without the right infrastructure, a single cross-border assignment can take months to arrange and expose the company to immigration, tax, and labor law risk in multiple jurisdictions simultaneously.

EXPAT PAYROLL STRUCTURES

Three payroll models. One will fit your assignment.

The structure of your expat payroll depends on where the legal employer sits and how long the assignment runs. Marco advises on and implements all three.

MODEL 01

Shadow Payroll

The employee stays on the home-country payroll. A shadow payroll is created in the host country to calculate and remit local taxes without actually paying the employee from there.

Best for: Short or medium-term assignments where the home employment relationship must remain intact.

MODEL 02

Host Payroll

The employee is fully transferred onto the host-country payroll and paid locally. Home-country payroll is suspended or terminated for the duration of the assignment.

Best for: Long-term assignments or permanent relocations where local employment status is required.

MODEL 03

Split Payroll

Part of the compensation is paid in the home country, part in the host country. Typically used when the employee has tax or benefit obligations in both jurisdictions simultaneously.

Best for: Assignments with dual tax residency, family obligations in both countries, or benefit continuity requirements.
