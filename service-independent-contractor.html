WHAT IS AN IC

Self-employed specialists. Engaged on your terms.

An independent contractor is a self-employed individual or registered business that provides paid services on a project or fixed-term basis. Unlike employees, ICs work for multiple companies simultaneously, set their own working methods, and are not bound by the same labor law obligations.

For businesses, IC engagement offers speed, flexibility, and access to highly specialized skills without the overhead of permanent headcount. The catch is compliance: misclassifying a worker as an IC when they should be an employee is one of the most common and costly HR legal risks globally.

THE COMPLIANCE PROBLEM

Misclassification is a serious risk

Most companies do not misclassify on purpose. The rules are just genuinely hard to get right. The consequences when you do not are significant.

WHY IT HAPPEN

Every country has its own rules

What counts as a contractor in Singapore may be employment in Germany. There is no universal standard.

The rules keep tightening

EU Platform Work Directive (2024), Netherlands enforcement active since Jan 2025, UK IR35, California AB5. Last year's compliant setup may not pass today.

Each country is a separate exposure

One contract template does not work globally. Every market where you have a contractor is its own compliance problem.

WHAT IT COSTS

Back taxes from day one

All unpaid employer taxes and social contributions owed from the first day of the engagement. Not just going forward.

Regulatory fines on top

Separate penalties from labor ministries, tax agencies, and social security bodies. Per worker, per jurisdiction.

Years of unpaid benefits

Reclassified workers can claim everything they should have received: paid leave, minimum wage top-ups, pension, health coverage.

Lawsuits and public proceedings

Individual complaints can become class actions. These are slow, public, and expensive to defend regardless of outcome.

The consequences apply retroactively. By the time you find out there is a problem, the liability has already been accumulating.
