Visa Guide Chile

Chile

Last Update by, 5 June 2026

1. Types of Work Visas

Chile's immigration system underwent a major reform after the new immigration law took effect in 2022, and is administered by the Servicio Nacional de Migraciones / SERMIG. The main work-related visa types are as follows:

Visa typeApplicable applicantsDescription
Temporary Residence Permit (Residencia Temporal)Foreign employees employed by a Chilean employerThe most common type; valid for 1–2 years and renewable; after the 2022 new law, it is no longer tied to a specific employer and the employee may change employer.
Permit for lawful paid activitiesProfessionals / independent contractorsApplies to foreigners with a local employment contract, job offer, or service contract; the contract must be submitted to SERMIG within 45 days after entry.
Investor / executive / specialist technician permitCorporate executives, investorsRequires an InvestChile support letter; applies to multinational transfers, executives, and key personnel in investment projects.
Multiple-entry permit for executives / directorsInternational executives who visit regularlyAllows multiple entries; stay in Chile may not exceed 6 months in each calendar year.
MERCOSUR residence permitCitizens of MERCOSUR member countriesApplies to citizens of Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay; valid for 2 years and renewable.
Working Holiday visaYoung people aged 18–30 (depending on treaty country)Limited to citizens of countries with agreements; valid for 12 months; dependants are not allowed.
Transitory Stay VisaDigital nomads / short-term visitorsAllows a stay of up to 90 days, extendable for another 90 days; the holder may not work for a Chilean company.
The following focuses on the most common "Temporary Residence Permit (lawful paid activities)", which accounts for more than 80% of Chile work authorizations.

2. Applicant Requirements

1. Basic eligibility

Has received a formal job offer or employment contract from a Chile-registered employer
Holds a valid passport (validity ≥ 12 months)
Has no criminal record
Is in good health
Salary requirement: monthly salary ≥ CLP 500,000 (around USD 515); technical roles usually USD 1,500–4,000

2. Employer-side conditions

A company legally registered in Chile (registered with SII)
Has a valid corporate RUT tax number
Issues an employment contract compliant with Chilean labor law
Recommended contract term ≥ 3 months
Important reminder
After the 2022 new immigration law, foreign employees must be employed and paid by a local Chilean entity. Salary may not be paid only by an overseas parent company.

3. Application Document Checklist

1. Employee documents

Valid passport (validity ≥ 12 months) and color scan of personal information page
Recent color passport-size photo (specification: 4.5cm × 3.5cm, white background)
Proof of legal residence in the country of origin
Bank statements for the most recent 3 months
Criminal record certificate (issued within 60 days before application, Apostille required)
Medical examination report (issued within 60 days before application by a licensed doctor, certification required)
Academic certificates, diplomas, and professional qualification certificates (Apostille required)
Personal CV (Spanish or English)
Travel explanation letter in English or Spanish (explaining all reasons for travel)

2. Employer documents

Employment contract or formal Offer Letter issued by the Chilean employer
Employee job description (Job Description)
Company registration document (Escritura de Constitución)
Company RUT certificate
Proof of employer financial capacity
Guarantee letter (undertaking obligations such as employee repatriation costs)
Tip
All non-Spanish or non-English documents require official translation; all documents issued outside Chile require Apostille.

Employee registers online for a ClaveÚnica electronic identity account
Submit the application through the SERMIG official portal, Portal de Trámites Digitales
Upload all documents (PDF format)
Pay the application fee online
Wait for SERMIG approval (6–8 months)
After approval, go to the local Chilean consulate to collect the visa label

During entry

The employee enters Chile with the temporary residence permit (90 days). The employee may start working after entry, but must complete the employment contract submission procedure within 45 days.

After entry

Register with the Policía de Investigaciones (PDI) within 30 days after entering Chile
Submit the signed employment contract to SERMIG within 45 days; the residence permit will automatically extend to 1 year
Apply for RUT (Chilean tax identification number, used for tax, banking, contract signing, etc.)
Within 30 days after residence permit approval, go to Registro Civil to apply for the foreigner ID card (Cédula)

5. Arrival Guide

Important reminder
Before obtaining the RUT number, the employee cannot open a bank account, sign a rental contract, or complete other legal procedures. It is recommended to apply immediately after entry.

1. Work-related

Register with Policía de Investigaciones (PDI) within 30 days after entry
Obtain the RUT tax identification number
Apply for the foreigner ID card (Cédula de Identidad)
Employer registers the employee for social security (AFP pension fund)
Register for health insurance (FONASA public healthcare or ISAPRE private healthcare)

2. Life-related

Open a local bank account (Banco de Chile / Santander / BCI / Banco Estado, etc.)
Rent housing (recommended platforms: Portal Inmobiliario, TocToc, Yapo, etc.)
Get a local SIM card (Entel / Movistar / Claro / WOM)
Register a BIP! card (Santiago public transportation card)

3. Family visas

Temporary Residence Permit holders may apply for a dependant permit (Visa de Dependiente) for spouses and minor children
The applicant must prove sufficient financial capacity to support dependants
The dependant visa validity is the same as the main applicant's permit
If dependants need to work, they must apply separately for work authorization

6. Existing Residence Card: Changing Employer

The 2022 new immigration law (Law No. 21,325) abolished the old "employer-tied permit" (Visa Sujeta a Contrato) and introduced the new "Permit for Remunerated Activities". For employees who already hold a Chile residence card and want to change employer, the rules have been significantly simplified.

1. Core rules

ItemRules under the new law
Relationship between work permit and employerThe new permit is no longer tied to a specific employer; the residence card exists independently of the employment contract.
Effect of contract terminationTermination of the employment contract no longer causes the residence card to become invalid automatically.
Changing employerThe employee may freely change employer without reporting to SERMIG.
ReapplicationNo need to reapply for a work permit or renew the residence card.
Return ticket clauseThe employer is no longer required to include a return ticket payment clause in the contract.

2. Practical steps for changing employer

Terminate the employment contract with the original employer (following the notice period required by Chilean labor law)
Sign a new employment contract with the new employer
The new employer registers the new contract with DT (Dirección del Trabajo)
Update employer information with the AFP pension fund and health insurance (FONASA/ISAPRE)
No need to update the residence card or notify SERMIG

3. Key differences from the old law

Old law (before 2022)New law (from 2022)
Visa Sujeta a Contrato tied to a specific employerResidence card is not tied to employer; the holder may change jobs with the card.
Visa became invalid within 30 days after contract terminationResidence card remains valid until the original expiry date.
Changing employer required reapplicationNo reapplication and no notification to SERMIG required.
Employer was responsible for return ticket costsEmployer is no longer mandatorily responsible for the return ticket.
Note
Although the residence card will not become invalid because of contract termination, employment stability is an important factor for permanent residence applications. When reviewing permanent residence, SERMIG will assess how long the employee had income security during the temporary residence period; this must cover at least more than half of the temporary residence period.

4. Renewal and permanent residence before residence card expiry

Temporary residence cards are usually valid for 1–2 years and may be renewed or converted to permanent residence before expiry
Renewal can be submitted in Chile through the SERMIG electronic portal
After holding a temporary residence card for 2 years, the employee may apply for permanent residence (Residencia Definitiva)
During renewal / permanent residence review, the residence card is automatically extended

7. Departure Guide

Terminate the employment contract (following the notice period required by Chilean labor law)
Within 30 days after contract termination, the residence permit becomes invalid automatically; during this period the employee may look for a new employer or arrange departure
Notify SERMIG of the departure plan
Complete tax settlement (SII process)
Close bank accounts
Cancel FONASA/ISAPRE health insurance
Return the foreigner ID card (Cédula)

8. 2022 New Immigration Law and 2025–2026 Key Points

Change itemContent
Employer unbindingThe new "Permit for Remunerated Activities" is no longer tied to a specific employer. Employees can change employer without reapplying for the permit.
Local employment requirementForeign employees must be employed and paid by a local Chilean entity. Salary may not be paid purely by an overseas parent company.
Fully digital applicationAll temporary residence permit applications are submitted through the SERMIG electronic portal.
Overseas applicationExcept for specific circumstances, applicants must submit their first application from outside Chile.
45-day contract submission deadlineAfter entering Chile, employees must submit the signed contract to SERMIG within 45 days, otherwise the residence permit will become invalid.
Approval backlogCurrent processing time is ~6–8 months, so advance planning is required.
Permanent residence pathAfter holding a temporary residence permit for 2 years, the applicant may apply for permanent residence, with time outside Chile during the year not exceeding 2 months.
