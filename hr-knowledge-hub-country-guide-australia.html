2. Salary Benchmark

Australia uses 121 Modern Awards for industry minimum wages. The NMW applies only to employees not covered by an Award (approx. 0.7%).

NMW national minimum wage: AUD 24.95 / hour (FY 2025–26 · +3.5% from July 2025)
Award industry minimums: Clerical L1 AUD 25.32 / hour · RN nurse AUD 39 / hour · Construction L1 AUD 28 / hour
Average weekly earnings: AUD 1,975 (gross · Nov 2025 ABS) · annualized AUD 102,700 · median annual salary approx. AUD 75,500

Total Additional Employer Cost (local employee · % of base salary)

Super 12% · WorkCover approx. 1 – 4% · Payroll Tax approx. 5% · Leave Loading approx. 0.5%

Mandatory Benefits / Common Benefits

Superannuation (SG): employer must contribute 12% of OTE monthly into the employee-nominated Super account
Annual Leave Loading: some Awards require an extra +17.5% pay when annual leave is taken (holiday penalty compensation)
Salary Sacrifice: employees may contribute pre-tax salary to Super (annual cap AUD 30,000 including SG)
Novated Lease: employer makes car lease payments on behalf of the employee through pre-tax salary deductions; significant tax effect

9. Key Regulations

Fair Work Act 2009 – core labour legislation
Federal unified law. Includes NES National Employment Standards (11 minimum entitlements) + Modern Awards (121 industry minimum wage instruments). Enforced by FWC and the Fair Work Ombudsman.
Closing Loopholes Act 2023–2024 – loophole-closing reforms
Right to Disconnect (Aug 2024) · casual-to-permanent conversion request right · platform gig worker rights · same job, same pay · action against Sham Contracting.
Payday Super (1 July 2026) – real-time retirement contributions
From July 2026, Super must be paid with every payroll cycle (previously quarterly). Non-compliance may trigger SGC interest + penalties + loss of tax deductibility. Employers need to upgrade STP systems.
Respect@Work + Sex Discrimination Act – proactive anti-sexual harassment duty
Since Dec 2022, employers have a Positive Duty to actively eliminate sexual harassment / sex discrimination. AHRC has investigation powers and high penalties may apply.
Privacy Act / Employee Records – employee privacy
2025 amendments restrict how employers process and transfer employee records. Data breaches must be reported to OAIC within 30 days. Individual penalties up to AUD 50,000.
WHS / Work Health & Safety – occupational health and safety
Federal + state harmonized legislation. Employers hold PCBU duties for workplace safety. 2024 Industrial Manslaughter offences introduced; psychosocial safety is included.
