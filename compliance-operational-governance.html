COMPLIANCE

Operational
Governance

Certifications tell you what Marco has achieved. Governance tells you how Marco operates every day — the controls, accountability structures, and audit trails that make those certifications meaningful.

OUR POSITION

Governance is not
a document.
It is how we operate.

Most compliance programs produce policies. Marco's governance framework produces accountability — defined roles, documented controls, and a clear chain of responsibility at every stage of service delivery.

Every payroll run, every client engagement, every internal process operates within a structure where no single person holds unchecked authority, and every action is traceable.

This is what enterprise clients require, and what Marco's three-pillar compliance framework is built to deliver.

COMPLIANCE FOUNDATION

Three pillars behind
every engagement

Marco's compliance framework is structured across three pillars that together cover legal, governance, and risk obligations in every market.

Business Compliance

Global operations meet local labor, tax, and data-protection requirements in every market Marco serves. Compliance is built into service delivery, not managed separately from it.

Corporate Governance

Transparent management framework with a clear organizational structure, defined separation of duties, and internal audit controls operating across all service lines and geographies.

Risk Management

Systematic identification, assessment, and mitigation of compliance and security risks across operations. Risk is not treated as a one-time exercise but as a continuous operational discipline.

INTERNAL CONTROLS

Audit, risk, and
policy controls

The operational controls that govern how Marco manages risk, maintains policy, and keeps every process auditable.

Policy Management

Operational policies are documented, version-controlled, and reviewed on a defined cycle. Every service line operates under a written policy framework.

  • Payroll processing procedures per entity
  • Client engagement and escalation policies
  • Data handling and retention policies

Risk Register

Compliance and operational risks are identified, assessed, and tracked. Risk management is an ongoing operational process, not a periodic review.

  • Jurisdiction-level compliance risk monitoring
  • Vendor and third-party risk assessment
  • Operational risk escalation procedures

Incident Management

Incidents are logged, classified, and tracked through to resolution. Each incident generates a documented record used to strengthen controls and prevent recurrence.

  • Structured incident classification and logging
  • Root cause analysis on all material incidents
  • Client notification procedures where applicable

Evidence & Audit Trail

Every material action across payroll, compliance, and client service generates an auditable record. Evidence is maintained and accessible for internal review and external audit.

  • Payroll approval and payment records
  • Access logs and change history
  • SOC 2 Type 2 and ISO 27001 audit evidence
