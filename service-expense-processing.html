THE PROBLEM

Global expenses are messier than they look.

Employees in different countries submit expenses in different currencies, against different tax rules, with different documentation requirements. What counts as a valid receipt in Germany is not the same as in China or Brazil.

Most companies handle this with spreadsheets, email chains, and a finance team that manually reconciles everything at month end. It is slow, error-prone, and creates real tax exposure when local compliance is missed.

Different rules in every country

Tax treatment, receipt requirements, and benefit-in-kind rules vary by jurisdiction. One global policy does not cover everything.

Manual processes cause delays

Employees wait weeks for reimbursement. Finance teams spend hours reconciling. Approvers do not know what to sign off on.

Tax errors create liability

Wrongly categorized expenses or missed gross-up requirements can trigger audits and back-tax assessments across multiple countries.

No visibility until month end

Finance teams have no real-time view of expense spend. Budget overruns are discovered after the fact, not before.
