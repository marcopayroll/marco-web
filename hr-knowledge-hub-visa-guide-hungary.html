Types of Work Visas

Hungarian immigration affairs are managed by the National Directorate-General for Aliens Policing (OIF / NDGAP, Orszagos Idegenrendeszeti Foigazgatosag). Since 1 March 2024, Hungary’s Act XC of 2023 on the Entry and Residence of Third-Country Nationals has fully taken effect and restructured the residence permit system. For non-EU / EEA / Swiss nationals, Hungary uses a Single Permit (Residence Permit for Employment Purposes) route, combining residence and work authorisation. The main 2026 work-related residence permits are:

Residence Permit TypeWho It Applies ToDescription (2026)
Single Permit / Residence Permit for Employment PurposesSkilled third-country nationals employed by a Hungarian employerMainstream route; residence and work are combined; first permit up to 3 years, renewable; included in the 35,000 annual quota; employer applies to OIF; family reunification allowed.
Hungarian Card / Magyar KártyaHigh-skilled / shortage-role employees hired by Hungarian strategic partner companiesDesigned to attract high-end talent; labour market test exempt; first permit up to 3 years, renewable; family reunification allowed; eligible for permanent residence.
EU Blue CardHighly educated + high-salary professionalsMonthly salary threshold: HUF 1,001,048 for regular roles / HUF 800,838 for shortage roles; 2026 standard; strict degree requirement; long-term EU mobility; up to 4 years.
Guest Worker Residence PermitLower-skilled guest workers from designated countries hired by designated employersIntroduced from 2024.3; limited to nationals of the Philippines, Georgia, and Armenia; first 2 years + 1-year extension (3-year total cap); no permanent residence and no family reunification.
Guest Investor PermitThird-country nationals making compliant investments in HungaryLaunched from 2024.7; EUR 250,000 real estate fund units or EUR 1 million donation to education / research; 10+10 years; no residence requirement; family members may accompany.
ICT – intra-corporate transferMultinational company managers / specialists / traineesManagers / specialists up to 3 years; trainees 1 year; may apply for EU ICT Mobile.
Family reunification permitSpouse + minor children of Single Permit / Blue Card / Hungarian Card holdersValid in line with the main applicant; spouse may work; not available for Guest Worker Residence Permit.

Focus of This Guide

The Single Permit (Residence Permit for Employment Purposes) is the main route for mid-level employment; the Hungarian Card / EU Blue Card are preferred routes for highly skilled talent. The Guest Worker route is limited by nationality and occupation scope (only the Philippines, Georgia, and Armenia), and cannot lead to permanent residence or family reunification. This guide focuses on the Single Permit, Hungarian Card, and EU Blue Card.

Applicant Requirements

1. Employee-side Requirements

(1) Single Permit / Residence Permit for Employment Purposes

Has signed a formal employment contract with a Hungarian registered employer.
Monthly salary is not lower than the industry average and not lower than the 2026 guaranteed minimum wage of HUF 373,200 for skilled / secondary-education roles, or the minimum wage of HUF 322,800.
Has the education or professional qualification required for the role (some roles require notarisation / Apostille + Hungarian translation).
Police clearance certificate (issued by Chinese public security authority + Apostille + Hungarian translation).
Health insurance proof (Schengen travel insurance before entry; after entry, registration into the Hungarian social security TB system).
Accommodation proof (rental contract / employer-provided accommodation statement).

(2) Hungarian Card

Employed by a company recognised by the Hungarian government as a strategic partner or by an employer in a shortage occupation.
High-skilled position / higher education or professional qualification matching the role.
Exempt from labour market testing (the employer does not need to prove that “no local candidate can be hired”).
Not subject to the 35,000 annual quota.

(3) EU Blue Card

Monthly salary threshold (2026): HUF 1,001,048 (≈EUR 2,565) for regular roles; HUF 800,838 (≈EUR 2,050) for shortage roles.
Bachelor’s degree or above (recognised through ENIC-NARIC or the Hungarian Educational Authority), or 5+ years of senior professional experience (3 years for IT roles).
Employment contract term >= 6 months.

(4) Guest Worker Residence Permit

Limited to nationals of 3 countries: the Philippines, Georgia, and Armenia (from 2025.1).
Employed by a company on the OIF “List of Trusted Employers”.
Performs occupations specified by the government (mostly manufacturing, construction, agriculture, hospitality).
Total stay capped at 3 years; no permanent residence / no family reunification.

2. Employer-side Requirements

The company must be registered in Hungary (Cegbirosag commercial registry + valid tax number).
No major tax violations, wage arrears, social security violations, or other negative compliance record.
Ordinary Single Permit requires a labour market test: the role is advertised with the national employment service for 15 days to prove that no suitable local candidate is available.
Hungarian Card / EU Blue Card / Trusted Employer / strategic partner: exempt from labour market testing.
The employer must submit the application through the EnterHungary electronic portal and accept OIF review.
For guest workers: the employer must be on the OIF “List of Trusted Employers” (certification required in advance).
Monthly social security and payroll declarations must be filed and paid in full (13% employer social contribution tax Szocho + payroll taxes); late payment or arrears may cause the employee permit to be revoked.

Important Reminder

The 2024 immigration law tightened several core rules. The 35,000 annual quota covers Single Permits + Guest Workers + other employment residence permits. Guest Workers have a 3-year total cap, cannot extend beyond it, cannot apply for family reunification, and cannot obtain permanent residence. Before accepting an offer, confirm whether the role is within the quota and whether the employer is on the OIF “List of Trusted Employers”.

Application Document Checklist

1. Employee Documents

Valid passport (remaining validity >= 18 months, at least 2 blank pages) + scanned biodata page.
ID photo (35×45mm, white background, taken within the last 6 months, Schengen-standard).
Birth certificate (Apostille + Hungarian translation; simplified after China joined the Hague Convention in 2023.11).
Education certificate + degree certificate (if required, recognised by ENIC-NARIC or the Hungarian Educational Authority).
Employment contract (position, salary, start date, contract term).
Police clearance certificate (issued in home country + Apostille + Hungarian translation, valid within 6 months).
Health insurance proof (Schengen travel insurance at entry + Hungarian social security TB registration after entry).
Accommodation proof (notarised rental contract / employer accommodation statement + owner consent).
Visa application form (D-type long-stay visa) + proof of application fee payment.
Marriage certificate / children’s birth certificates (if family members accompany, Apostille + translation required).

2. Employer Documents

Company commercial registry excerpt (Cegkivonat, issued within the last 30 days).
Valid tax number certificate + proof of tax and social security payments for the past year.
Employment contract signed and stamped by the employer, including salary details, position, and start date.
Job description (work duties, required qualifications, salary range).
Labour market test result (if applicable): 15-day unsuccessful posting certificate issued by the national employment service.
If applying as a “Trusted Employer” or “strategic partner”: relevant certification document.
EnterHungary electronic portal online application form + system-generated application number.
OIF application fee payment proof (Single Permit ≈HUF 90,000; Hungarian Card / Blue Card ≈HUF 110,000–130,000; Guest Worker permit ≈HUF 70,000).

Tip: Apostille and Hungarian Translation

Hungary is strict about “Apostille + translation”. Birth certificates / marriage certificates / degree certificates / police clearances must first be Apostilled in the issuing country (China can issue Apostille directly from 2023.11), then translated into Hungarian by the Hungarian Office for Translation and Attestation (OFFI) or an OFFI-authorised translator. English-only translations are generally not accepted; a formal Hungarian translation is required.

Detailed Steps

1.The employer opens a corporate account on the EnterHungary electronic portal (enterhungary.gov.hu). If applicable, the employer advertises the role with the national employment service for 15 days to complete the labour market test. Exempt cases include: Hungarian Card, EU Blue Card, companies on the List of Trusted Employers, and strategic partners.
2.The employee obtains police clearance, education notarisation, and birth certificate Apostille in the home country; documents are translated into Hungarian by OFFI or an authorised translator.
3.The employee books an appointment with the Hungarian embassy / consulate / VFS center in the consular district, submits the D-type long-stay visa + Single Permit combined application, provides biometrics (fingerprints + photo), and pays the visa fee of EUR 110.
4.The embassy forwards materials to OIF. The administrative timeline is 21 working days, with a statutory cap of 70 days (including correction periods). Guest Worker and Hungarian Card cases are faster, usually 4–6 weeks.
5.OIF approval → the embassy notifies the employee to issue the D-type entry visa (visa sticker), valid for 30 days; the employee must enter within this period.
6.Within 3 working days after entry: register the address at the local OIF regional office and submit the residence card photo; OIF usually mails the residence card within 8–15 working days.
7.Register the tax number (Adoazonosito jel / TIN) with the National Tax and Customs Administration (NAV); register the social security number (TAJ kartya) with the health insurance fund (NEAK); the employer also declares the employee in NAV.
8.Bank account + SIM card setup: open an account using passport, residence card, TAJ, and address proof (lakcimkartya).

Tip: Processing Timeline

OIF’s statutory cap is 70 days, but ordinary Single Permit cases are usually completed within 6–10 weeks; Hungarian Card / Trusted Employer cases may be shortened to around 4 weeks. It is strongly recommended to complete all Apostille + Hungarian translations in China before arrival, because supplementary processing after arriving in Hungary is slow and costly.

Tip: Annual Quota Timing

The 35,000 quota is released in monthly batches; applications in low seasons (December / July) are often easier to approve.

Arrival Guide

Important Reminder: Registration and TAJ After Entry

After entering with a D-type visa, registration with the local OIF regional office must be completed within 3 working days, otherwise residence card issuance may be affected or fines may be imposed. The residence card (tartozkodasi kartya) is usually mailed to the registered address within 8–15 working days after registration. The TAJ social security number is the core document for medical care, reimbursement, and tax matters, and must be completed within 2 weeks after entry.

1. Work-Related

Within 3 working days after entry: register the residence address at the local OIF regional office and submit the residence card photo.
Within 5 working days after entry: register the tax number (TIN) at NAV → receive the Adoazonosito jel card immediately.
Within 15 days after entry: apply for the social security number (TAJ kartya) at the NEAK health insurance fund / government office window.
Employer completes employee declaration in the NAV system → employer withholds 15% personal income tax + 18.5% employee social security and pays 13% employer social contribution tax Szocho.
Personal income tax: Hungary uses a flat 15% tax rate, applying to worldwide income for tax residents or Hungary-source income for non-residents.
Family tax allowance (csaladi adokedvezmeny): monthly allowance per child in 2026 is HUF 66,670 for 1 child, HUF 133,330/month for 2 children, and HUF 220,000/month for 3+ children.
If planning permanent residence: after 3 years of continuous lawful residence + meeting requirements (language / income / no criminal record), the person may apply for national residence permit (nemzeti letelepedesi engedely); citizenship generally requires 8 years (3 years if through marriage).

2. Daily Life

Mandatory health insurance: covered through NEAK / TAJ under the national health insurance system; basic medical care is covered. Private supplementary insurance is optional (Generali, Allianz, Aegon).
Open a local bank account: OTP, K&H, Erste, Raiffeisen, UniCredit, CIB, Revolut (digital bank); passport + residence card + address proof + tax number are required.
Rent housing: use ingatlan.com, alberlet.hu, Facebook Marketplace; central Budapest 1-bedroom is around EUR 600–900/month; deposit is usually 2 months.
SIM card: Yettel, Vodafone, Telekom, One; prepaid (feltoltokartyas) / postpaid options available; passport is sufficient to purchase.
Transport: Budapest BKK monthly pass for bus / metro / tram / train is around HUF 9,500; MAV trains are nationwide; student monthly passes under age 25 are cheaper.
Mobile payment: Revolut, Wise, SimplePay are common; most merchants accept UnionPay / Visa / Mastercard contactless payment; in smaller cities cash + instant bank transfer (QR code) still exists.
Driver licence: a Chinese driver licence may be converted to a Hungarian licence within 1 year after arrival (health check + Hungarian translation required), with no road test; after 1 year, the driving test must be retaken.

3. Dependent Visas

Spouses + minor children of Single Permit / Hungarian Card / EU Blue Card holders may apply for a family reunification residence permit (csaladegyesitesi tartozkodasi engedely).
The spouse may work freely after entry (no separate work permit required).
Children: unmarried children under 18 may reunify; residence permit must be obtained within 3 months.
Parents / parents-in-law: may apply only if the main applicant has a support obligation and can prove sufficient financial resources; review is strict.
Family members must also register at the local OIF regional office within 3 working days and collect residence card + TAJ after arriving in Hungary.
Guest Worker Residence Permit holders cannot apply for family reunification; spouses and children cannot accompany them for residence.

Existing Residence Permit: Changing Employers

Hungary’s Single Permit / Blue Card / Hungarian Card is tied to a specific employer. When the employee changes employer, the new employer must submit a modification of employer application to OIF. The employee cannot work for the new employer before OIF approval. The Guest Worker Residence Permit is fully tied to the employer at issuance and is generally not changeable.

1. Core Rules

Residence Permit TypeEmployer Change Rule
Single PermitEmployer may be changed, but OIF approval is required; the new employer must complete labour market testing unless exempt; the employee cannot work for the new employer during the modification period.
Hungarian CardNew employer must be a strategic partner / Trusted Employer; exempt from labour market testing; OIF processing usually 2–4 weeks.
EU Blue CardWithin the first 12 months, changing employer requires reapplying for the Blue Card; after 12 months, only notification is required.
Guest Worker Residence PermitGenerally cannot change employer; if change is needed, a new visa must be applied for and the employee must leave and re-enter.
ICT intra-corporate transferICT is tied to the sending company; during the Hungarian stay, transfer into a local Hungarian company is not allowed, only group-internal transfer.
National permanent residenceFully free, with no employer restriction.

2. Practical Steps to Change Employers

1.Employee signs an employment contract with the new employer; confirm the new employer’s qualification + whether the labour market test is exempt.
2.The new employer submits the “modification of employer” application through the EnterHungary electronic portal, uploading the new contract + company commercial registration + tax certificate.
3.If required, the new employer first advertises the role with the national employment service for 15 days to complete labour market testing.
4.OIF decides within 2–6 weeks. During the modification period, the employee cannot officially work for the new employer (contract signing is allowed, but onboarding / paid work is not).
5.The former employer must report the termination of employment through the NAV system + notify OIF within 5 days after the actual termination date.
6.Unemployment grace period: in principle, the residence permit remains valid until the card expiry after the former employment contract ends, but the employee should find a new employer and start a new application within 15 days; otherwise OIF may revoke the permit.
7.If the validity period changes, OIF mails a new residence card.

Important Reminder: No Work During Modification Period

The key risk in changing employer under a Single Permit is that the employee cannot officially work for the new employer during the modification application period (no onboarding / paid work), otherwise it is illegal employment and may lead to residence permit revocation and deportation. Ideally, the “modification of employer” application and OIF approval should be completed before the original employment contract ends. Guest Worker Residence Permit holders cannot change employer mid-way and must leave and re-enter with a new permit.

4. Permanent Residence and Employer Change

Single Permit / Blue Card / Hungarian Card holders who have continuously and lawfully resided for 3 years (5 years in some cases) and meet requirements such as language (A2), stable income, no criminal record, and health insurance may apply for a national residence permit (Nemzeti letelepedesi engedely) or EC long-term residence permit. Permanent residence removes employer binding entirely, allowing free employment / self-employment / entrepreneurship. After 5 years, the holder may apply for Hungarian citizenship (3 years through marriage). The Guest Worker route cannot lead to permanent residence.

Departure Guide

1.Submit resignation notice according to the employment contract (felmondasi ido, usually 30–90 days); employer completes final payroll settlement + unused holiday compensation.
2.Employer reports “termination of employment” (kilepes) in the NAV system; social security / tax relationship terminates at the same time.
3.Employer also reports employee departure to OIF through the EnterHungary electronic portal.
4.Go to the local OIF regional office to cancel the residence card + return the card (required by some offices).
5.Complete tax clearance with NAV: income for the departure year must be declared; over-withheld tax may be refunded.
6.Social security (TB / TAJ): contribution records remain in the system; China and Hungary do not yet have a comprehensive social security agreement, so pension contributions generally cannot be transferred out. Keep the TAJ card for any future return to Hungary.
7.Close bank account: recommended to keep it open for 1–2 months to settle final automatic debits; clear all standing orders + credit cards before closure.
8.Cancel subscriptions: phone / internet contracts usually require 30 days’ notice; transfer electricity / gas / water contracts to the landlord or new tenant.
9.Move out: recover rental deposit (initial move-in condition record is needed); complete property inspection (atadas-atvetel).
10.Deregister address (lakcimkartya): complete move-out registration (kijelentkezes) at the local government office.
11.If the employee may return to Hungary in the future: keep the tax number (TIN) + TAJ (lifetime valid) + Ugyfelkapu (e-government identity) account for remote tax matters.

Key Policy Changes from 2023 to 2026

Hungary has adjusted in two directions: “controlling lower-skilled migration” and “attracting high-skilled talent / capital investment”. 2024 brought the largest immigration law reform in nearly a decade, and multiple new rules are in effect in 2026:

New immigration law effective 2024.3.1Act XC of 2023 on the Entry and Residence of Third-Country Nationals fully took effect; residence permit categories were restructured: Single Permit, Hungarian Card, Guest Worker, Guest Investor, etc.
Guest Worker system (2024.3)New Guest Worker Residence Permit + Qualified Guest Worker Permit; initially up to 2 years + 1-year extension, total cap 3 years; no permanent residence and no family reunification.
Guest Worker country list tightened (2025.1)Countries eligible for Guest Worker permits were reduced from the initial 17 to 3: the Philippines, Georgia, and Armenia.
35,000 annual quota (2025.1)Single Permit + Guest Worker + employment-related residence permits share a combined annual cap of 35,000 permits; released monthly; Hungarian Card / Blue Card are outside the quota.
Guest Investor Residence Permit launched (2024.7)Investment amount EUR 250,000 (real estate fund units) or EUR 1 million (donation). From 2025.1, the EUR 500,000 direct property purchase option was cancelled; 10+10 years of residence.
EU Blue Card salary threshold increased (2026)Regular roles: HUF 1,001,048 / month (≈EUR 2,565); shortage roles: HUF 800,838 / month (≈EUR 2,050); applies to all valid Blue Card holders.
