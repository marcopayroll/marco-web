Salary Benchmark

Spain uses a 14-salary system: 12 monthly salaries + double payments in June and December. Annual salary is approximately 17% higher than monthly salary after conversion.

  • SMI minimum wage: EUR 1,184 in 2025. In 2026, raised by 3.1% to EUR 1,221 / month under RD 1/2026.
  • From 2026, SMI employees are fully exempt from IRPF, but social security contributions still apply normally.
  • Average annual salary: EUR 28,950 gross in 2025 according to INE.
  • Median annual salary: EUR 24,300.
  • Madrid, Barcelona, and the Basque Country lead salary levels.

Employer add-on total cost (local employees · as % of base salary)

Common risks 23.6% · unemployment 5.5% · FP 0.6% · FOGASA 0.2% · MEI 0.67% · work accident ~1.5%. Employer total add-on cost is ~32–36% of base salary · the 14-salary system adds another +17% to annual total cost.

Mandatory Benefits / Common Benefits

  • 14-salary system: 12 monthly salaries + July summer double salary + December Christmas double salary. Also known as paga extra. Statutory.
  • Supplementary healthcare (Mutua / Adeslas / Sanitas): commonly provided by mid-sized and large companies through private hospital networks.
  • Meal vouchers (Ticket Restaurant / Sodexo): not mandatory. EUR 11 / day is tax-free, with an annual maximum of EUR 2,816.
  • Supplementary retirement plan (Plan de Pensiones): provided by some large companies, usually 1 – 5% of base salary.

Personal Income Tax

IRPF is progressive. It includes the national scale and autonomous-community scale, and regions may adjust slightly. Employers withhold monthly. Annual filing is due before June 30.

  • Capital gains / savings income: 19 – 30% under a separate tax table, with 6,000 / 50K / 200K / 300K brackets.
  • IVA: 21% general rate. Food is 4 – 10%. Finance and healthcare are 0%.
  • Non-resident (NRA, residence < 183 days per year): flat 24%, or 19% within the EU. No tax allowances.
  • Beckham Law (Régimen Impatriado): foreign professionals enjoy a 24% flat rate for the first 6 years, up to EUR 600K.

Overtime Pay (Horas Extra)

Reference: ET Art. 35.

  • Structural overtime: hourly rate × 125%, or equal time off. Usually, the convenio provides for time off.
  • Emergency overtime (Fuerza Mayor): does not count toward the annual 80-hour cap and must be reported to the labour authority afterwards.
  • Digital time recording (Registro Horario): mandatory since May 2019. Daily clock-in / clock-out records must be kept for 4 years.
  • From 2025, the government has promoted a 37.5-hour weekly working-time reform. It is still under consultation in 2026.

Dismissal Protection and Compensation

  • Despido Objetivo: 20 days' salary / year, capped at 12 monthly salaries. Requires economic, technical, organisational, or production reasons.
  • Despido Improcedente: 33 days' salary / year, capped at 24 monthly salaries. Applies from 2012; old contracts used 45 days.
  • Despido Nulo: reinstatement + full back pay, including pregnancy, union, and discrimination scenarios.
  • Notice period: Despido Objetivo requires 15 days' written notice. Failure to notify requires an additional 15 days' salary.

Key Regulations

Estatuto de los Trabajadores (RDL 2/2015) – Core Labour Law

Current core labour law. It covers working hours, wages, dismissal, leave, and unions. It is enforced jointly by SEPE and Inspección de Trabajo.

RDL 32/2021 – 2022 Labour Reform

The maximum penalty for abuse of temporary contracts is EUR 187,500. The reform legalised Fijo-discontinuo seasonal contracts and prioritised collective agreements.

Ley Rider (RDL 9/2021) – Platform Gig-worker Law

Delivery and food-delivery riders are presumed to be employees by default. Social security is required. Algorithm transparency is mandatory, with heavy penalties for violations.

Beckham Law / Régimen Impatriado – Foreign Talent Tax Incentive

Foreign professionals relocating to Spain enjoy a 24% flat tax rate for the first 6 years, up to EUR 600K. Foreign assets are exempt. The regime supports multinational transfers.

Registro Horario Digital – Digital Time Clocking

Since May 2019, all employees must clock in and out daily. Employers must keep records for 4 years. Penalties range from EUR 6,250 – 187,500.

Ley de Igualdad / Plan de Igualdad – Gender Equality Law

Since March 2022, companies with 50 or more employees must have an equality plan. Requirements include equal pay and an anti-sexual-harassment mechanism. Penalties can reach up to EUR 225,000.
