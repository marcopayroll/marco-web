Types of Work Visas

South Korea’s immigration system is mainly handled by the Ministry of Justice through the Korea Immigration Service (KIS / HiKorea). Employment-related visas also involve the Ministry of Employment and Labor (MOEL). The main work-related visas in 2026 are as follows:

Visa Type Who It Applies To Description (2026)
E-7-1 Specific Activities Professionals in 67 designated occupations (management, IT, R&D, design, research, etc.) Main white-collar work visa. 2026 minimum salary: KRW 31,120,000. Usually a master’s degree; or a bachelor’s degree + 1 year of related experience. Initial stay 1–3 years, renewable up to 5 years. Route to F-2-7 / F-5.
E-7-4 Skilled Workers Skilled workers in manufacturing, welding, foundry, agriculture, fisheries, shipbuilding, and other core industries 2025 quota: 35,000. E-9 workers with 5 years in Korea + Korean language + employer recommendation may switch. Stay up to 5 years. Possible F-2 route.
E-9 EPS General Employment Non-professional foreign labor recruited from EPS source countries 2025 quota: 130,000. Recruited under the Employment Permit System (EPS). Maximum stay: 4 years 10 months. Reform discussions for 2026 may allow freer job changes after 1–2 years.
D-8 Business / Investment Foreign investors establishing or investing in Korean companies D-8-1 requires at least 10% shareholding. D-8-4 tech start-up track uses a points system. Initial stay 1 year, renewable up to 5 years.
D-10 Job Seeker / Internship Highly educated foreigners seeking work or paid internships in Korea D-10-1 is points-based. D-10-T exempts master’s / doctoral graduates from world Top 100 universities. 6 months + 6 months, up to 2 years. No formal full-time work.
Top-Tier Talent (E-7-T / F-2-T / F-5-T) Elite global talent in advanced industries Launched in 2024. E-7-T salary must be at least 3× Korea’s per-capita GNI (about KRW 130–140 million). F-2-T may lead to F-5-T after 3 years. Spouses may work freely.
F-2-7 Points-Based Residence Skilled residents already in Korea on D-series or E-series visas Total score: 170; 80 points needed. Holders may change employers freely. Valid 3 years and renewable. Important route toward F-5 permanent residence.
F-3 Dependent / Family Spouses and unmarried children under 18 of eligible visa holders Valid for the same period as the principal visa. F-3 holders may reside in Korea but cannot take full-time employment without additional permission.

Focus of This Guide

E-7-1 is the main route for foreign professionals. E-9 EPS and E-7-4 are more focused on blue-collar and core-industry labor. The following sections focus mainly on E-7-1, E-7-4, and the Top-Tier route.

Applicant Requirements

1. Employee-Side Requirements

(1) E-7-1 Specific Activities

  • The job must fall within the Ministry of Justice list of 67 E-7-1 “Specific Activities” occupations.

  • Qualification: related master’s degree; or bachelor’s degree + at least 1 year of related experience; or 5+ years of experience in the same field.

  • 2026 minimum annual salary: KRW 31,120,000. In practice, KRW 48 million or above is safer.

  • No general mandatory Korean-language rule, but TOPIK 3+ may be needed in some sectors such as education or healthcare.

  • Passport validity should be at least 6 months; no serious criminal record.

(2) E-7-4 Skilled Workers

  • For E-7-4: total stay in Korea of 5 years on E-9 / E-10 / H-2.

  • Korean language: TOPIK 3 or KIIP level 4 or above.

  • Annual salary must be at least 80% of the previous year’s per-capita GNI (about KRW 28 million in 2026).

  • Employer recommendation and skills assessment are required.

(3) Top-Tier / D-10 Job Seeker

  • For Top-Tier E-7-T: master’s / doctoral degree from a world Top 100 university plus at least 8 years of experience, including 3+ years at a Top 500 company.

  • Salary must be at least 3× Korea’s per-capita GNI (about KRW 130–140 million in 2026).

  • For D-10 job-seeking: D-10-1 uses a general points system; D-10-T gives a privileged route to Top 100 university graduates. Job searching and short paid internships are allowed, but not full-time employment.

2. Employer-Side Requirements

  • The company must be legally registered in Korea and have no major tax or labor-law violations.

  • For E-7-1, Korean employees must generally account for at least 80% of the workforce (foreign workers <= 20%).

  • The employment contract must state salary, job title, working hours, and social-insurance arrangements.

  • The employer should prove the role is not easily replaceable, usually by a 14-day Work-Net posting or special technical justification.

  • E-9 employers must first obtain an Employment Permit from MOEL.

  • Top-Tier employers should be in designated advanced sectors such as semiconductors, biotech, AI, batteries, or quantum.

Important

The 80% Korean-employee ratio is a hard E-7 threshold. Many small companies and start-ups are refused on this basis. Salary-payment compliance is also closely checked: wages should be paid into the employee’s own Korean bank account.

1. Employee Documents

1.

Valid passport + bio-page copy.

2.

Passport photo (35×45mm, white background).

3.

Academic degree / diploma (Apostille + Korean translation).

4.

Employment / experience proof (Apostille + Korean translation).

5.

Employment contract in Korean.

6.

CV / resume + academic transcript.

7.

Criminal-record certificate.

8.

Health check report (including TB and drug test).

9.

For E-7-4: TOPIK / KIIP results + skills assessment.

10.

Marriage / birth certificates for dependents (if any).

2. Employer Documents

1.

Corporate registration + business registration copy.

2.

Last 3 years’ tax-payment certificates + financial statements.

3.

Employment contract including salary, hours, position, benefits, and insurance.

4.

Job description + proof of non-substitutability (Work-Net posting, patents, technical explanation).

5.

Organization chart + employee roster showing the Korean-staff ratio.

6.

Proof of enrollment in the four major social-insurance systems.

7.

For E-9: Employment Permit + standard labor contract + departure / return-cost insurance.

8.

Fees: CCVI KRW 30,000; embassy / consulate issuance USD 60 single or USD 90 multiple.

Document Note

Korea is strict about Apostille + Korean translation. Academic records, criminal records, birth certificates, and marriage certificates typically require Apostille before Korean translation.

Application Process

Process Overview

Step Action Party Time
1 Post the role on Work-Net for 14 days. Employer 14 days
2 Sign the labor contract. Employer / Employee 1–2 weeks
3 Submit the CCVI application via HiKorea / immigration office. Employer Immediate
4 Immigration review. Immigration Office 2–6 weeks
5 Apply for visa issuance at the Korean embassy / consulate using the CCVI number. Employee 5–10 business days
6 Enter Korea. Employee Upon visa issuance
7 Apply for the Alien Registration Card (ARC) within 90 days. Employee Within 90 days
8 Enroll into social insurance / health insurance. Employer / Employee After arrival / month 6

Detailed Steps

1.

The employer first posts the position on Work-Net for at least 14 days to show it cannot easily be filled locally. E-9 workers are allocated through EPS.

2.

The employer and employee sign the Korean-language labor contract. E-7 salary should meet at least KRW 31,120,000 in 2026.

3.

The employer files for the CCVI (Certificate for Confirmation of Visa Issuance) through HiKorea or the competent immigration office.

4.

Once approved, the employee applies at the Korean embassy / consulate using the CCVI number.

5.

Entry usually must take place within 3 months from CCVI issuance.

6.

Within 90 days after arrival, the employee must apply for the ARC (Alien Registration Card).

7.

The company then completes enrollment in National Pension, NHIS health insurance, Employment Insurance, and Industrial Accident Compensation Insurance.

HiKorea Note

HiKorea is the central online immigration platform. For most E-series applicants, the CCVI route is the standard and safer route before applying for visa issuance at the embassy or consulate.

Landing Guide

Important

After entering Korea on a valid work-status visa, apply for the ARC within 90 days. Without the ARC, routine matters such as banking, telecom, housing, and healthcare become difficult.

1. Work-Related Matters

1.

Apply for the ARC within 90 days after entry. The ARC is essential for banking, housing, telecom, medical visits, and contracts.

2.

Social insurance: National Pension 4.75% employee + 4.75% employer; Health Insurance about 3.55%; Employment Insurance 0.9% employee; Industrial Accident Insurance employer-paid.

3.

NHIS usually applies automatically after 6 months of residence, although company coverage often starts earlier for employed E / D visa holders.

4.

Personal income tax is progressive at 6%–45%. Eligible foreign employees may choose the 19% flat tax regime during the first 5 years.

5.

For permanent residence: E-7-1 usually needs 5 years in Korea; Top-Tier 3 years. F-2-7 is a major transition route.

6.

Extensions may be filed through HiKorea from 4 months before expiry up to 1 day before expiry.

2. Daily-Life Matters

1.

Bank accounts: KB Kookmin, Shinhan, Hana, Woori, KakaoBank, or Toss Bank; usually ARC + passport + proof of employment required.

2.

Housing platforms: Naver, Zigbang, Dabang. Common rental models are Jeonse and Wolse. Many foreigners prefer Wolse.

3.

Mobile service: SKT, KT, LG U+. Budget MVNO plans are cheaper. ARC is normally required.

4.

Transport: T-Money card + subway / bus / taxi / KTX. WOWPASS is useful for transport and currency exchange.

5.

Popular mobile payments: KakaoPay, Naver Pay, Toss, Samsung Pay.

6.

Driver’s license exchange may be possible with a home-country license + Apostille + Korean translation.

3. Family Visas

  • Spouses and unmarried children under 18 may apply for F-3 dependent visas.

  • F-3 holders generally cannot work full-time unless they obtain separate work permission or switch status.

  • Top-Tier spouses may receive F-2-T1, which gives full work freedom.

  • Dependents also need an ARC within 90 days. NHIS usually requires 6 months’ residence before dependent enrollment, subject to some exemptions.

  • Required documents usually include the marriage / birth certificate, the principal’s employment certificate, and proof of funds.

Existing Visa: Changing Employers

Korean E-7 and E-9 work visas are generally employer-specific. If the employee changes employers, a new filing is usually required. E-9 job changes are tightly restricted. F-2-7 and F-5 holders are not employer-bound.

1. Core Rules

Visa Type Changing Employer
E-7-1 New employer must also satisfy the 80% Korean-staff rule. A HiKorea status / employer change and usually a new CCVI process are required.
E-7-4 Usually must move within the same industry, with employer recommendation and change filing.
E-9 EPS Only limited statutory reasons permit a change. Maximum 3 times. Same-region and same-industry restrictions apply.
D-8 Tied to the investor’s own company; key changes must be reported to immigration.
D-10 Not tied to an employer. Switch to an E-series visa once a qualifying job is found.
F-2-7 / F-2-T / F-5 Not employer-bound. Holders may change jobs freely.

2. Practical Steps

1.

Sign the new labor contract and confirm the new employer meets E-7 conditions.

2.

If required, post the role on Work-Net for 14 days.

3.

File the employer-change / status-change application through HiKorea with the new contract and company documents.

4.

Immigration review usually takes about 2–4 weeks; most E-7 cases do not require leaving Korea.

5.

The former employer reports termination within 15 days; the new employer reports commencement within 15 days.

6.

If only the employer changes, ARC validity often continues. If the visa category changes, a new ARC is issued.

3. Key Timing Comparison

Item E-7-1 E-9 EPS
New employer requirement 80% Korean staff + sound finances Valid Employment Permit
Number of changes No formal cap Maximum 3
Regional / industry restriction None Same region and same industry
Grace period after unemployment About 1 month in practice Must find a new employer within 3 months
Need to exit Korea? Usually no Usually no
2026 salary threshold KRW 31,120,000 Government EPS wage level (about KRW 20.6 million)
Former employer duty Report termination within 15 days Report termination and assist with return formalities if required

E-7 Grace Period Note

For E-7 holders, there is no clear statutory grace period after job loss, but in practice a new employer should normally be secured within about 1 month or the person should move to another suitable status such as D-10.

4. Permanent Residence and Employer Changes

E-7-1 holders normally need 5 years of lawful residence to seek F-5 permanent residence; Top-Tier holders may qualify after 3 years. F-2-7 is a major stepping-stone because it removes employer dependence and may later lead to F-5.

Departure Guide

1.

Give written resignation notice based on the contract or at least 30 days in advance for open-ended contracts. The employer should settle final salary and severance pay.

2.

The employer reports employment termination through HiKorea within 15 days. For E-9 / E-7-4, MOEL also needs notice.

3.

National Pension (NPS): a lump-sum refund may be possible if the worker qualifies under an applicable bilateral social-security arrangement.

4.

NHIS: request termination before departure; overpaid premiums may be refundable.

5.

Complete the year-end tax settlement or authorize the employer to do it.

6.

Return the ARC if required; proactive return is recommended.

7.

Close bank accounts only after all card charges and automatic payments clear.

8.

Cancel telecom, internet, broadband, and subscription services in writing.

9.

Keep HiKorea account details if a future return to Korea is possible. Some long-term residents should also check re-entry rules carefully.

10.

Declare cash exceeding the equivalent of USD 10,000 and keep purchase documents for high-value goods.

Key Policy Changes for 2024–2026

South Korea has recently tried to attract more high-end talent while also expanding skilled-worker pathways. The main policy changes up to 2026 are below:

Policy Change Content
E-7-1 Salary Threshold Increase Raised from KRW 30,840,000 in 2025 to KRW 31,120,000 in 2026.
Expansion of E-7-4 Quota The skilled-worker quota rose sharply, and the route from E-9 to E-7-4 became easier.
Top-Tier Launch and Expansion E-7-T / D-10-T / F-2-T / F-5-T were introduced; spouses on F-2-T1 may work freely.
F-2-7 Points-System Update Total score remains 170; 80 points are needed. Additional points apply in some situations.
E-9 Reform Discussion (2026) Reforms may allow freer job changes after 1–2 years, though current restrictions still apply.
Higher E-9 Quota 2025 annual quotas increased across several categories including E-9 and E-7-4.
NHIS Dependent-Enrollment Rule F-3 dependents usually need 6 months of residence before enrollment, subject to exemptions.
National Pension Rate Increase Employee and employer contributions rose to 4.75% in 2026.
F-2-R Regional Visa A route for residents and workers in designated population-decline areas.
F-1-D Workation Visa For remote workers employed by overseas employers; local Korean work is not allowed.
Annual Visa-Quota Notice System Authorities publish annual visa quotas by industry and category.
China Apostille Simplification Chinese documents can now be used with Apostille, without older double legalization.
