Types of Work Visas

Spanish immigration matters are mainly managed by the Foreigners Office (Oficina de Extranjería) under the Ministry of the Interior and the Large Companies and Strategic Groups Unit (UGE-CE) under the Ministry of Inclusion, Social Security and Migration. For non-EU / EEA / Swiss nationals, the main work routes are Highly Qualified Professional (HQP) and the regular work permit (Cuenta Ajena). HQP uses the Law 14/2013 fast-track route and is approved by UGE-CE; Cuenta Ajena follows the Ley de Extranjería and requires labour-market testing. The following are the main work-related residence permits in 2026:

Residence Permit Type Who It Applies To Description (2026)
Highly Qualified Professional (HQP) (Law 14/2013) High-salary foreign employees working for large companies / strategic groups Main fast-track route; UGE-CE approval in ~20 working days; salary threshold: technology / intellectual roles €40,077, management / executive roles €54,142, under age 30 reduced to €30,500; valid up to 3 years; renewable.
EU Blue Card (Tarjeta Azul-UE) Highly educated + high-salary professionals Annual salary €39,269.92 (1.4× average wage); management roles / graduates within 3 years reduced to €31,415.94; bachelor degree or 5 years professional experience; valid 3 years, renewable for 2 years.
Cuenta Ajena Regular Work Permit General employees who are not high-salary / not HQP Employer must prove the role is in the shortage occupation list (Catálogo de Ocupaciones de Difícil Cobertura) or pass labour-market testing; approval 1–3 months; initial 1 year, renewal up to 4 years.
ICT Intra-Company Transfer (Law 14/2013) Managers / specialists / trainees transferred inside a multinational group Managers / specialists up to 3 years; trainees 1 year; UGE-CE fast track; EU ICT allows assignments within the EU.
Entrepreneur Visa (Visado Emprendedor / Startup Visa) Entrepreneurs developing an innovative project in Spain Under Law 28/2022; ENISA issues innovation certification, then UGE-CE approves; valid 3 years, renewable for 2 years; dependents may accompany.
Researcher Visa PhD holders or researchers with a research contract Law 14/2013 Article 38; requires a hosting agreement with a public or private research institution; valid 3 years.
Non-Lucrative Visa Non-working residents with sufficient financial resources Important: holders may not legally work in Spain; used only for passive-income residents and not covered as a work visa in this guide.

Main Routes Covered in This Guide

HQP and the EU Blue Card are Spain’s two main routes for attracting high-end talent. Both are processed by UGE-CE through a fast-track system (~20 working days). The following sections focus on HQP and the EU Blue Card. Investor residence (Golden Visa) was formally abolished on 3 April 2025 and no new applications are accepted.

Applicant Requirements

1. Employee Side

(1) Highly Qualified Professional (HQP)

  • Salary threshold (2026): technology / intellectual / technical roles €40,077; management / executive roles €54,142; under age 30 reduced to €30,500.

  • Only fixed salary counts toward the threshold; bonuses / variable pay are usually not counted.

  • Education requirement: university bachelor degree or above, or at least 3 years equivalent professional experience.

  • Police clearance certificate from country of origin + countries of residence in the past 5 years, with Hague Apostille certification.

  • Full health insurance coverage, either private insurance or public social security coverage.

  • No mandatory Spanish-language requirement, but long-term residence / nationality later involves DELE A2.

(2) EU Blue Card (Tarjeta Azul-UE)

  • Salary threshold €39,269.92 (about 1.4× average wage, 2026); management roles / graduates within the last 3 years €31,415.94.

  • Bachelor degree or above, or at least 5 years relevant professional experience. IT roles may be treated flexibly under EU Directive 2021/1883.

  • Degree recognition (Homologación / Equivalencia) through the Ministry of Universities (Ministerio de Universidades).

  • Employment contract term ≥ 6 months.

(3) Entrepreneur Visa (Visado Emprendedor)

  • The project must obtain innovation and economic-interest certification from ENISA (Empresa Nacional de Innovación).

  • Company must be under 5 years old (biotech / energy / industrial projects may be extended to 7 years), annual revenue < €10 million, and not listed.

  • Applicant must be a founder or core member involved in management and strategic decisions.

2. Employer Side

  • HQP / EU Blue Card: employer must be a Spain-registered company. Large companies with 250+ employees or high-turnover companies automatically meet HQP qualification; SMEs must prove strategic-industry / innovative status.

  • Employer must have no Social Security (TGSS) or tax (Hacienda) arrears and no serious labour violations.

  • Regular Cuenta Ajena employers must prove the role is in the shortage occupation list (Catálogo de Ocupaciones de Difícil Cobertura), or first pass a 25-day labour-market test through SEPE.

  • Employment contract must meet the salary and working-hour standards under the relevant collective agreement (Convenio Colectivo).

  • Employer must register the employee with Social Security (TGSS) under the General Regime (Régimen General). Employer social security is about 30%; employee side about 6.35%.

  • Any contract change / resignation must be reported through the social security system within 10 days.

Cuenta Ajena Work Permit Restrictions

The regular Cuenta Ajena work permit is restricted by “Situación Nacional de Empleo” (national employment situation). Unless the role is in the shortage occupation list or the employer can prove no Spanish / EU candidate can do the job, approval is difficult. Most high-salary employees should use HQP or EU Blue Card to avoid Cuenta Ajena. Before accepting an offer, confirm whether the employer can use the UGE-CE route.

Application Document Checklist

1. Employee Documents

  • Valid passport (remaining validity ≥ 1 year) + scanned personal information page.

  • Passport photos (Spain biometric standard 32×26mm, white background, colour, 3 copies).

  • Birth certificate (Hague Apostille + translation into Spanish by an official sworn translator, Traductor Jurado).

  • Degree certificate + transcript (Apostille + Traductor Jurado translation; EU Blue Card requires Homologación / Equivalencia through the Ministry of Universities).

  • Police clearance certificate (country of origin + countries of residence in the past 5 years, Apostille + Traductor Jurado; issued within 3 months).

  • Employment contract copy (salary, position, start date, contract term).

  • Health insurance proof (private insurance may be used temporarily for HQP / EU Blue Card; after arrival, transition to Spanish social security Sistema Nacional de Salud).

  • CV and position-matching explanation.

  • Marriage certificate / children’s birth certificates if dependents accompany (Apostille + Traductor Jurado).

  • Visa application form (consular “Solicitud de Visado Nacional”) + visa fee payment receipt.

2. Employer Documents

  • Company documents: CIF tax number and Commercial Registry (Registro Mercantil) extract.

  • Employment contract signed by employer, with detailed salary, position and work location.

  • Social Security compliance certificate (TGSS Certificado de estar al corriente).

  • Tax compliance certificate (Hacienda Certificado de estar al corriente).

  • Company profile: turnover, number of employees and organisation chart (large HQP companies are automatically exempt).

  • For Cuenta Ajena: SEPE labour-market test result / proof that the role matches the shortage occupation list.

  • For HQP / EU Blue Card: UGE-CE online application form (Solicitud de Autorización de Residencia).

  • Fees: UGE-CE Modelo 790-052 approx. €78 (HQP residence permit) + visa fee approx. €80 for Chinese citizens.

Tip — Apostille + Traductor Jurado Translation

Spain is strict about “Apostille + Traductor Jurado (official sworn translator) translation”. Birth certificates, marriage certificates, degree certificates and police clearance certificates must first be Apostilled in the issuing country, then translated into Spanish by a Traductor Jurado certified by the Spanish Ministry of Justice. Foreign notarised translations or general certified translations are not accepted. Since China joined the Hague Apostille Convention on 7 November 2023, Chinese public documents can be used after direct Apostille, without the previous dual authentication through the Chinese MFA + Spanish Embassy in China.

Application Process

Process Overview

Step Action Responsible Party Estimated Time
1 Employer and employee sign the employment contract + prepare documents. Employer / Employee 1–2 weeks
2 Employer submits HQP / EU Blue Card / ICT / Entrepreneur application through the UGE-CE portal. Employer Online submission immediately
3 UGE-CE review (HQP / EU Blue Card / ICT / Entrepreneur). UGE-CE Approx. 20 working days (HQP)
4 Cuenta Ajena route: employer submits the application to Oficina de Extranjería. Employer Approval 1–3 months
5 After approval, employee applies for the entry visa (Visado Nacional) at the Spanish embassy / consulate within 1 month. Employee Approx. 10 working days
6 Employee enters Spain (visa valid for 90 days). Employee Arrival day
7 Within 3 months after arrival: register residence at the local city hall (Ayuntamiento) and obtain Empadronamiento. Employee Within 3 months
8 Within 30 days after arrival: attend fingerprint appointment at Extranjería / Comisaría de Policía and apply for the TIE foreigner identity card. Employee Apply within 30 days; card issued in 30–45 days
9 TGSS social security registration + employment contract takes effect. Employer / Employee Before start date

Detailed Steps

  • Employer and employee sign the employment contract. The employee prepares the Apostille + Traductor Jurado document package; the employer prepares company documents and social security / tax compliance certificates.

  • HQP / EU Blue Card / ICT / Entrepreneur visa: employer logs into the UGE-CE portal and submits the “Solicitud de Autorización de Residencia”, uploading all employee and company documents; dependents can be applied for at the same time.

  • UGE-CE usually issues a decision within 20 working days. If there is no response after 20 working days, the application is deemed approved by “silencio administrativo positivo” (positive administrative silence) for HQP / EU Blue Card / ICT.

  • For the Cuenta Ajena regular work permit route: employer submits the application to the provincial Oficina de Extranjería where the position is located. Approval takes 1–3 months, during which SEPE checks the labour market.

  • After approval, the employee must book an appointment within 1 month at the Spanish embassy / consulate with jurisdiction (or VFS / BLS centre), submit the original passport + Apostille documents + visa form, provide biometrics, and receive the visa in about 10 working days.

  • Employee enters Spain with the entry visa (valid for 90 days). No additional border endorsement is required; residence status starts from the date of entry.

  • Within 3 months after arrival, the employee must register at the local city hall (Ayuntamiento) for Empadronamiento, using passport + visa + proof of address (lease / property deed / landlord statement), and obtain the Certificado de Empadronamiento (valid for 3 months).

  • Within 30 days after arrival, book a “toma de huellas” (fingerprinting) appointment at Extranjерía or Comisaría de Policía; submit EX-17 form + Modelo 790-012 fee receipt (approx. €16.29) + Empadronamiento + residence approval; collect the physical TIE card after 30–45 days.

  • Employer completes TGSS social security registration (Alta en Seguridad Social) before the employee starts work. The employee obtains a social security number (NUSS) using the NIE and is automatically included in the General Regime (Régimen General).

  • If Beckham Law (Régimen Especial de Impatriados) applies, the employee must submit Modelo 149 to Agencia Tributaria within 6 months after starting work; after approval, a 24% flat tax applies for 6 tax years.

Tip — Spanish Work Visa Appointments for Chinese Citizens

Chinese citizens applying for a Spanish work visa use BLS Spain Visa centres (Beijing / Shanghai / Guangzhou / Shenyang / Hangzhou / Wuhan / Chengdu) for consular appointments; advance booking is required. After China joined the Hague Apostille Convention, all public documents can be Apostilled directly, without the previous “Chinese MFA + Spanish Embassy in China” dual authentication process.

Arrival Guide

TIE and Empadronamiento Deadlines

After entering with a national entry visa (Visado Nacional), the employee may legally start working. However, within 30 days after arrival, the employee must attend fingerprinting at Extranjería and apply for the TIE foreigner identity card. Within 3 months, the employee must also complete Empadronamiento at the city hall. Without Empadronamiento, almost all later steps — TIE, bank account, healthcare registration and children’s school enrolment — will be blocked.

1. Work-Related

  • Within 3 months after arrival: register at the local city hall (Ayuntamiento) for Empadronamiento → obtain Certificado de Empadronamiento immediately (valid 3 months; often the first key identity document).

  • Within 30 days after arrival: apply for TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) at Extranjería / Comisaría de Policía; fee €16.29; card issued in 30–45 days.

  • Social security: automatically included in the General Regime (Régimen General); employer side approx. 30%, employee side approx. 6.35% (including pension 4.7%, unemployment 1.55%, wage guarantee 0.1%).

  • Apply for Beckham Law (Régimen Especial de Impatriados): submit Modelo 149 within 6 months after starting work. Once approved, Spanish-sourced employment income up to €600,000 is taxed at a flat 24% for 6 tax years; foreign income is exempt.

  • Personal income tax: Hacienda automatically registers the tax relationship. 2026 general IRPF progressive rates: first bracket (≤ €12,450) 19%, second (≤ €20,200) 24%, third (≤ €35,200) 30%, fourth (≤ €60,000) 37%, fifth (≤ €300,000) 45%, sixth (> €300,000) 47%.

  • If planning for permanent residence: after 5 years of continuous legal residence, the employee may apply for long-term residence (Residencia de Larga Duración). Nationality usually requires 10 years, plus DELE A2 Spanish + CCSE culture exam.

2. Daily Life

  • Healthcare: after TGSS registration, the employee enters public healthcare (Sistema Nacional de Salud). Use Tarjeta Sanitaria to access the Centro de Salud. Private insurance (Sanitas, Adeslas, DKV) is about €40–80/month.

  • Local bank account: BBVA, Santander, CaixaBank, Sabadell, ING, N26, Revolut, etc. Usually requires passport + NIE + Empadronamiento + employment contract / salary proof.

  • Housing: platforms include Idealista, Fotocasa, Habitaclia and Pisos.com. Madrid / Barcelona / Valencia are competitive; deposit is usually 1–2 months + 1 month prepaid rent + agency fee (agencia) around 1 month.

  • SIM card: Movistar, Vodafone, Orange, Yoigo, MásMóvil, Lowi, Lobster; prepaid / postpaid available, usually requires NIE + Empadronamiento.

  • Transport: Madrid / Barcelona metro monthly passes approx. €20–40; high-speed rail (AVE / RENFE) connects major cities; since 2024, RENFE Cercanías commuter rail has provided large subsidies for frequent travellers.

  • Mobile payment: Bizum is Spain’s mainstream instant bank transfer system (receive money by phone number; covers 90% of merchants and P2P use). Credit / debit card acceptance is high; cash usage continues to decline.

3. Dependent Visas

  • Spouse / cohabiting partner, including Pareja de Hecho and same-sex marriage, may apply for family reunification (Reagrupación Familiar) together with the main applicant.

  • Spouse / partner of HQP / EU Blue Card / Entrepreneur visa holders has full access to the labour market after entry, without a separate work permit; self-employment and entrepreneurship are also allowed.

  • Children: unmarried children under 18 may reunite. Under RD 1155/2024, dependent children under 26 may also reunite.

  • Parents / parents-in-law: dependent parents of HQP / EU Blue Card / Entrepreneur visa holders may apply if economic dependency and co-residence can be proven.

  • Dependents must also complete Empadronamiento + TIE application after arrival. The TIE card states “Familiar de Residente”.

Existing Residence Permit: Changing Employers

Spanish HQP / EU Blue Card / Cuenta Ajena residence permits are tied to a specific employer and position. When changing employers, the employee must apply for a modification of work and residence authorisation (Modificación de Autorización de Residencia y Trabajo). HQP and EU Blue Card holders may change employers more freely after holding the original permit for 12 months, but reporting and re-approval are still required. Entrepreneur visas are not tied to an employer but are tied to the approved project. Long-term residence (Residencia de Larga Duración) is fully unrestricted.

1. Core Rules

Residence Permit Type Employer Change Rule
HQP (Law 14/2013) Within the first 12 months, the new employer must apply to UGE-CE for “Modificación”; after that, changes must be reported and the new employer must still meet HQP standards (large company or strategic sector).
EU Blue Card Within the first 12 months, change of employer requires a new application and UGE-CE approval; after that, change only needs to be reported to UGE-CE; new employer must still meet the salary threshold.
Cuenta Ajena Regular Work Permit Tied to specific position and employer; new employer must apply again through Oficina de Extranjería and pass labour-market testing or prove shortage occupation.
ICT Intra-Company Transfer ICT is tied to the sending company; during ICT, the employee cannot transfer into a Spanish local company and may only move within the group in the EU (EU ICT Mobile).
Entrepreneur Visa (Visado Emprendedor) Not tied to employer but tied to the ENISA-approved innovation project; substantial project changes require reassessment.
Long-Term Residence (Residencia de Larga Duración) Fully free, no employer restriction; holder may work in any position in Spain.

2. Practical Steps to Change Employers

  • Employee signs an employment contract with the new employer; confirm that the new employer meets HQP / EU Blue Card standards and that salary reaches the 2026 threshold.

  • New employer submits “Modificación de Autorización de Residencia y Trabajo” on the UGE-CE portal, uploading the new contract + salary proof + company materials.

  • UGE-CE decides within 20 working days; positive administrative silence applies if the deadline passes for HQP / EU Blue Card.

  • Original employer must report “Baja en Seguridad Social” through TGSS within 10 days after the employee’s actual departure.

  • Unemployment grace period: HQP / EU Blue Card holders may remain legally in Spain and look for work for up to 3 months after losing employment. After 3 months with no new employer, they must leave Spain or switch to another visa, such as Búsqueda de Empleo.

  • Collect a new TIE card if the visa validity or category changes; attend fingerprinting again at Extranjería.

3. Key Timing Comparison

Item HQP EU Blue Card
New employer qualification Large company / high turnover / strategic sector Employer only needs to meet EU Blue Card salary threshold
Minimum initial contract term No clear rule (recommended ≥ 1 year) ≥ 6 months
Unemployment grace period 3 months 3 months
Change within first 12 months Requires UGE-CE re-approval Requires UGE-CE re-approval
Change after 12 months Report to UGE-CE only Report to UGE-CE only
Annual salary threshold (general) €40,077 €39,269.92
Annual salary threshold (management / executive) €54,142 €39,269.92 (no age distinction)
Annual salary threshold (under 30 / recent graduate) €30,500 €31,415.94

3-Month Unemployment Grace Period

The HQP / EU Blue Card unemployment grace period is 3 months, calculated from the date TGSS receives the original employer’s “Baja en Seguridad Social”. If no new employer is found during this period, the residence permit may be revoked and the employee must leave after the period expires. It is recommended to reach agreement with the new employer and start the UGE-CE modification application before the original employment contract ends. Cuenta Ajena holders do not benefit from the UGE-CE fast track, so changing employers is significantly harder and slower than for HQP / EU Blue Card.

4. Long-Term Residence and Employer Change

HQP / EU Blue Card / Cuenta Ajena holders may apply for long-term residence (Residencia de Larga Duración) after 5 years of continuous legal residence. For EU Blue Card holders, periods of residence in other EU member states may count. Long-term residence fully removes employer and position restrictions and allows free employment / self-employment / entrepreneurship. After continued residence of 10 years in most cases, or 2 years for nationals of Latin America / the Philippines / Equatorial Guinea / Portugal / Andorra / Sephardic Jews, nationality may be available with DELE A2 + CCSE culture exam.

Departure Guide

  • Submit resignation notice according to the employment contract (preaviso). The statutory minimum is 15 days; many sectors require 1 month. Employer completes final salary settlement + payment for unused annual leave (vacaciones no disfrutadas).

  • If the termination is economic redundancy / objective dismissal, severance compensation (indemnización) applies: objective reasons = 20 days salary per year of service, capped at 12 months; unfair dismissal = 33 days salary per year, capped at 24 months.

  • Employer processes “Baja en Seguridad Social” through TGSS within 10 days.

  • Deregister from the local city hall (Ayuntamiento) through Baja Padronal, using flight ticket or new address proof.

  • Hacienda tax filing: submit Modelo 100 (annual IRPF return) for the departure year. If using Beckham Law, terminate the special tax status according to law.

  • Social security contributions (pension / unemployment): accumulated by years and retained in the system after departure. China and Spain have a social security agreement effective from 2018; it may allow partial social security transfer or avoidance of double contributions.

  • Close Spanish bank account: keep it for 1–2 months to settle final Bizum / SEPA direct debits; before closing, settle all regular payments such as phone, internet, rent and insurance.

  • Cancel subscriptions: private health insurance / phone / internet contracts usually require 1 month written notice. Public healthcare (SNS) ends automatically with social security deregistration.

  • Return TIE card if required by the relevant Extranjería. Keep NIE, which is valid for life and useful for future Spain procedures.

  • If likely to return to Spain: keep Cl@ve digital identity (PIN / Permanente) + digital certificate (Certificado Digital) for remote tax, refund and document checks.

Key Policy Changes from 2023 to 2026

In recent years, Spain has balanced “attracting high-end talent and innovation / entrepreneurship” with “tightening investment immigration and reshaping the residence system”. Several new rules took effect in 2025–2026:

Change Item Details
Golden Visa Investment Residence Abolished (2025.4.3) Organic Law 1/2025: the €500,000 property-based Golden Visa was fully abolished. No new applications are accepted from 3 April 2025. Investors and dependents whose applications were already submitted keep their existing rights.
Major Immigration Regulation Reform RD 1155/2024 (effective 2025.5.20) Reglamento de Extranjería fully updated: initial residence permit 1 year, renewals up to 4 years; arraigo residence requirement reduced from 3 years to 2 years; students may work 30 hours/week; new socio-laboral arraigo and segunda oportunidad arraigo categories.
EU Blue Card Salary Threshold Increased (2026) General threshold €39,269.92 (about 1.4× average wage); management / graduates within 3 years €31,415.94; IT experts with 3 years experience may be exempt from degree requirement.
HQP Salary Threshold Increased (2026) Technology / intellectual roles €40,077; management / executives €54,142; under age 30 €30,500.
Ley de Startups (Law 28/2022) Implemented (2023.1) Entrepreneur visa requires ENISA innovation certification. Company < 5 years old (biotech / energy / industrial < 7 years), revenue < €10 million. Beckham Law extended to entrepreneurs and digital nomads.
Beckham Law Optimised (2023.1 / 2024 Adjustments) Prior foreign residence requirement reduced from 10 years to 5 years. Dependents (spouse + children under 25) may also benefit from the 24% flat tax. For the first 6 years, Spanish-sourced income up to €600,000 is taxed at 24%; excess is taxed at 47%.
Family Reunification Expanded (RD 1155/2024) Age limit for dependent children raised from 21 to 26. Non-EU family members of Spanish citizens receive 5-year residence + work permit; family members have labour-market access immediately after arrival.
Digital Identity Strengthened (Cl@ve / Certificado Digital) Since 2024, most Extranjería / tax / social security / city hall procedures can be completed online using Cl@ve PIN or digital certificate. TIE renewals can be applied for online.
China Apostille Simplified (2023.11.7) China formally joined the Hague Apostille Convention. Chinese public documents (birth certificate / marriage certificate / degree certificate / police clearance) may be used in Spain after direct Apostille, without dual authentication by the Chinese MFA + Spanish Embassy in China.
2024 Regularisation Policy In November 2024, Spain announced plans to regularise 900,000 irregular migrants over the next 3 years. In parallel, HQP / EU Blue Card holders have clearer compliance and renewal pathways.
