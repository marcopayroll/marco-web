2. Salary Benchmark

Wage system - no statutory minimum wage

Denmark has no statutory minimum wage; wages are set by industry collective agreements (Overenskomst).
CBA average minimum hourly wage is ~DKK 130 / hour (~€17.4); covers ~80% of the workforce.
Foreign worker salary threshold scheme (Pay Limit Scheme): annual salary must reach DKK 514,000 (2026).

Employer social security total is ~DKK 10,000 - 15,000 / year (very low · social security is mainly tax-funded).
Employees only contribute ATP DKK 1,188 / year + 8% AM tax (labour market contribution).
Employer additional total cost (including social security + holiday pay + pension): ~15–20% of gross wage (fixed social contributions ~2% + holiday pay 12.5% + CBA pension ~8–15%).

Mandatory benefits / common benefits

Holiday pay (Feriepenge): 12.5% of gross pay (blue-collar) or normal salary + 1% holiday supplement (white-collar).
Sick leave: employer pays 100% salary for the first 30 days; then municipal reimbursement (Sygedagpenge max DKK 4,865 / week).
Pension: set by CBA (usually employer 8–12% + employee 4–6%); not statutory but nearly universal.
Lunch subsidy / fitness / transport: common benefits; some are tax-exempt.

4. Personal Income Tax

Progressive system: bottom tax 12.01% + middle tax 7.5% + top tax 7.5% + top-top tax 5% + municipal tax ~25%. AM tax (Arbejdsmarkedsbidrag): 8% of gross salary (deducted before all other taxes).

Overtime pay

Overtime pay is set by CBA or employment contract (law does not prescribe a uniform rate).
Common CBA standard: first 3 hours +50%; afterwards +100%; weekends / holidays +100%.
Management employees (Funktionær) usually have no overtime pay, as it is included in salary.

9. Key Regulations

Salaried Employees Act (Funktionærloven)
Enacted in 1938; covers white-collar employees; regulates notice periods, sickness absence, non-compete clauses and more; one of Denmark's core employment laws.
Collective agreement system (Overenskomst)
Trade unions and employer associations negotiate every 2–3 years; covers ~80% of the workforce; sets practical standards for wages / working hours / benefits, etc.
Flexicurity model
Denmark's unique model: flexible dismissal + generous unemployment benefits + active labour-market policies; a global labour-market benchmark.
Holiday Act (Ferieloven · 2020 reform)
Concurrent holiday system (Samtidighedsferie); leave can be used as it is accrued; 12.5% holiday pay is deposited into FerieKonto.
EU member state + foreign work permits
EU / EEA citizens may work freely; third-country nationals must apply for work and residence permits (Pay Limit / Positivlisten, etc.).
