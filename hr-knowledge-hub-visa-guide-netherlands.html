Visa Guide Netherlands

Netherlands

Last Update by, 16 June 2026

1. Types of Work Visas

Immigration matters in the Netherlands are handled by the Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND), while labour-market review is handled by the Employee Insurance Agency (UWV). For non-EU/EEA/Swiss nationals, the main routes are the Highly Skilled Migrant route (HSM / Kennismigrant) and the EU Blue Card. The employer must first be an IND Recognised Sponsor.

Residence Permit TypeWho It Applies ToDescription (2026)
HSM Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant)High-salary foreign employees working for an IND Recognised SponsorMost common route. Monthly salary threshold (excl. holiday allowance): under 30 years old €4,357; 30 years and over €5,942; Orientation Year / Zoekjaar to HSM €3,122. Valid up to 5 years; may lead to permanent residence.
EU Blue CardHighly educated + high-salary professionalsMonthly salary threshold €6,107 (2026 estimate, based on 1.5× the social average wage). Strict degree requirement; supports long-term EU mobility; valid for 4 years.
GVVA combined residence + work permitNon-HSM general employeesApplies to stays over 90 days. Employer must pass UWV labour-market test. Processing may take up to 90 days. Valid for 3 years.
TWV work permitEmployees staying 90 days or under, or employees who already hold another residence permitIssued separately by UWV. Valid up to 1 year. Employer must pass labour-market test for each application.
ICT intra-company transferInternal transfer of multinational managers / specialists / traineesManagers / specialists up to 3 years; trainees 1 year. Company must be an IND Recognised Sponsor; ICT Mobile can be used for EU assignments.
Orientation Year (Zoekjaar)Recent graduates within 3 years from eligible universities, including THE / ARWU / QS global top 200 universities12-month job-search period. Free employment without HSM salary threshold. After finding HSM employment, the lower €3,122 threshold may apply.
Self-employed / EntrepreneurEntrepreneurs / self-employed persons in the NetherlandsAssessed under a points-based system. Start-up capital and business plan requirements are strict.

HSM (Kennismigrant) is the most common high-skilled route, accounting for more than 70% of foreign employee visa applications. The following focuses on HSM and the EU Blue Card.

2. Applicant Requirements

Employee Side — Highly Skilled Migrant (HSM)

Salary threshold (2026, monthly salary excluding 8% holiday allowance): under 30 years old €4,357; 30 years and over €5,942; Orientation Year graduate €3,122.
Annual salary must be "sustainable for 12 months", meaning the contract period and employment stability can cover 12 months.
Monthly salary must be verified through "SV-loon" (social insurance wage base).
No mandatory degree requirement (unlike the EU Blue Card), but the employer may set its own requirement.
No mandatory Dutch-language requirement, but permanent residence / naturalisation stage requires A2.

Employee Side — EU Blue Card

Monthly salary threshold €6,107, approximately 1.5× the social average wage (2026 estimate).
Bachelor’s degree or higher, or equivalent professional qualification, with Nuffic recognition.
Employment contract term of at least 6 months.

Employee Side — Orientation Year (Zoekjaar)

Obtained a bachelor’s / master’s degree in the Netherlands within the last 3 years, or graduated from a global THE / ARWU / QS top 200 university.
Application must be submitted within 1 year from the application eligibility date; late applications are not accepted.
Free employment for 12 months without the HSM salary threshold, suitable as a transition period for job searching.

Employer Side

Must be an IND Recognised Sponsor. Companies that are not sponsors cannot apply for HSM / Blue Card / ICT.
Company must be registered with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce (KvK), have healthy financial statements for the most recent year, and have no major compliance violations.
Pay the one-off sponsor registration fee: 2026 standard €4,294; companies with fewer than 50 employees pay half.
From 1 January 2026, in addition to payslips, employers must keep proof that the salary was actually paid into the employee’s own bank account, such as bank statements or batch payment lists.
Monthly salary must be paid on time and must not fall below the threshold. Salary below threshold immediately triggers HSM invalidity risk.
Employment contract / salary change / departure must be reported through the IND business portal within 4 weeks.
Important Reminder
Recognised Sponsor is the “gatekeeper” of the HSM route. If the employer loses recognition due to compliance failure or financial deterioration, the residence permits of all current HSM employees may be revoked. Before accepting an offer, the employee must confirm whether the employer appears in the IND public register of recognised sponsors (publiek register erkende referenten).

3. Application Document Checklist

Employee Documents

Valid passport (remaining validity at least 6 months) + scanned personal information page.
Passport photo meeting Dutch biometric photo standard 35×45mm, consistent with Schengen standard.
Birth certificate, with Hague Apostille + translation into Dutch / English / French / German.
Academic certificate — if applying for EU Blue Card, Nuffic / IDW recognition is required.
Employment contract, including salary, position, start date, and contract term.
Health insurance proof: Schengen travel insurance before entry, then switch to Dutch basic health insurance (Zorgverzekering) after arrival.
Tuberculosis test exemption / report: exempt if from an IND-listed exempt country; other countries must complete GGD test within 3 months after entry.
Marriage certificate / child birth certificate if dependents accompany, requiring Apostille + translation.
MVV visa application form if from an MVV-required country + visa fee payment receipt.

Employer Documents

IND Recognised Sponsor number (Erkenningsnummer).
KvK Chamber of Commerce registration extract issued within the last 3 months.
Employment contract signed by employer, including salary breakdown, position, and start date.
Job description (Functieomschrijving).
Proof of 12-month salary sustainability, such as employer financial summary, turnover, or order backlog.
Online HSM application form submitted by employer through the IND Business Portal.
From 1 January 2026: employee’s own bank account proof, used to verify salary payment compliance.
Application fee €423 for HSM / Blue Card / ICT / GVVA single application (2026 standard).
Tip
The Netherlands has strict “Apostille + translation” requirements. Birth certificates, marriage certificates, and academic certificates must be Apostilled first, then translated by a Dutch registered translator (beëdigd vertaler) into Dutch / English / French / German. Chinese-issued public documents can be Apostilled in China (simplified after China joined the Hague Convention in November 2023), then translated by a Dutch certified translator.

4. Application Process

Process Overview

StepActionResponsible PartyEstimated Time
1Employer becomes an IND Recognised Sponsor if not already recognised.EmployerNew application approx. 3 months
2Employer and employee sign the employment contract.Employer / Employee1–2 weeks
3Employer submits HSM application through IND Business Portal, including MVV entry visa request if applicable.EmployerOnline submission, immediate
4IND reviews HSM / EU Blue Card / ICT application.IND2–4 weeks; GVVA up to 90 days
5After approval, employee collects MVV at Dutch embassy / VFS in the responsible consular district if applicable.Employee1–2 weeks
6Employee enters the Netherlands within MVV 90-day validity.EmployeeArrival day
7Within 5 days after entry: register in BRP at the local municipality (gemeente) and obtain BSN.EmployeeWithin 5 days

Detailed Steps

If the employer is not yet recognised, it must first apply to IND for Recognised Sponsor status by submitting financial statements, business plan, and compliance undertakings online. The cycle is around 3 months.
Employer creates the HSM application in the IND business portal (zakelijk.ind.nl), uploads the employee passport page, employment contract, salary details, and Apostilled birth certificate; the MVV entry visa request may be selected at the same time.
IND usually decides HSM cases within 2–4 weeks. After approval, the “Notification of Decision” is sent to the employer and consulate. If MVV is required, the employee visits the Dutch embassy / VFS center in the relevant consular district for biometrics and MVV sticker collection.
Employee enters the Netherlands with MVV, valid for 90 days. No additional border endorsement is required; residence permit status starts from the date of entry.
Within 5 days after entry, the employee must register in the Personal Records Database (BRP) at the local municipality (gemeente): passport + MVV / residence card + address proof + Apostilled birth certificate — BSN (citizen service number) is issued immediately.
Within 2 weeks after entry, collect the physical residence card (verblijfsdocument) at the designated IND service desk. The card contains the BSN, visa type, and validity period.
For the 30% ruling (30%-regeling), employer and employee jointly apply to Belastingdienst within 4 months after entry. If approved, it applies retroactively to the employment start date.
Tip
MVV-exempt countries include the United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, the United Kingdom, etc. Employees from other countries, including China, must first collect the MVV entry visa at the embassy. Tuberculosis testing: employees from IND-listed low-risk countries (including China, Japan, South Korea, and many developed countries) are exempt; others must complete a chest X-ray test at GGD within 3 months after entry.

5. Arrival Guide

Important Reminder
After entering with a valid HSM / Blue Card residence permit, the employee may work legally. However, BRP registration and BSN must be completed at the municipality within 5 days after entry — otherwise bank account, health insurance, rental, and almost all follow-up procedures will be blocked. The residence card must be collected at the IND service desk within 2 weeks.

Work-Related

Within 5 days after entry: register in BRP at the local municipality (gemeente) — BSN is issued immediately.
Within 2 weeks after entry: collect the physical residence card (verblijfsdocument) at the IND service desk.
Social insurance: automatically integrated into the Dutch system, including AOW pension, WW unemployment benefit, and Zvw healthcare contribution; employer and employee together carry approximately 27.65% payroll social security, including 6.95% employer-paid items.
Apply for the 30% ruling (30%-regeling): employer submits a joint application with the employee to Belastingdienst within 4 months after employment starts. If approved, 30% of salary may be treated as tax-free reimbursement (still 30% in 2026).
Personal income tax registration: Belastingdienst automatically registers the tax relationship. 2026 Box 1 wage tax rates: first bracket (up to €38,441) at 35.82%, second bracket (up to €76,817) at 37.48%, third bracket at 49.5%.
If planning for permanent residence: after 5 consecutive years of legal residence + passing the integration exam (Inburgering) A2 / B1 + continuous employment, the employee may apply for permanent residence (Niederlassungsvergunning).

Daily Life

Mandatory health insurance (Zorgverzekering): Dutch basic health insurance (Basisverzekering) must be purchased within 4 months after entry. Basic premium is around €145–165/month in 2026; employer may provide Zorgtoeslag support.
Open a local bank account: ING, ABN AMRO, Rabobank, Bunq, N26, etc. Required documents usually include BSN + BRP proof + passport + residence card.
Housing: use Funda, Pararius, Kamernet, Holland2Stay, etc. Randstad areas such as Amsterdam / Rotterdam / The Hague / Utrecht are tight; deposit is usually 1–2 months.
SIM card: KPN, Vodafone, T-Mobile, Odido, Lebara. Prepaid / postpaid are both possible; BSN is needed.
Transport: OV-chipkaart (personal) + NS trains, tram / bus / metro nationwide. From 2026, some lines support direct bank-card tap-in.
Mobile payments: iDEAL is the dominant instant bank transfer method for online payments (around 90% online payment share). Tikkie (launched by ABN AMRO) is common for peer-to-peer collection. Credit card acceptance is lower than in many Western markets.

Dependent Visas

Spouse / cohabiting partner, including registered partner (Geregistreerd Partnerschap) and same-sex marriage, may apply for family reunification (Verblijf bij Partner).
Spouse / partner of HSM / Blue Card holder has full open labour-market rights upon entry and does not need a separate work permit. Self-employment and entrepreneurship are allowed.
Partner language exemption: nationals from Europe / North America / Australia / New Zealand / Japan / South Korea / UK / Israel, and partners of HSM / Blue Card holders, are exempt from the overseas A1 Dutch exam; other third-country partners must pass Basisexamen Inburgering before entry.
Children: unmarried children under 18 may reunify and must obtain a residence permit within 3 months.
Parents / parents-in-law: generally not allowed, except in very special circumstances, such as elderly living alone with the only direct family member in the Netherlands.
Dependents must also complete BRP registration and obtain BSN within 5 days after arrival.

6. Existing Residence Permit: Changing Employers

The Dutch HSM residence permit is tied to the specific employer. When changing employers, the new employer must be an IND Recognised Sponsor and must apply for a new HSM residence permit. The new employer must notify IND and complete the renewal / new application within 4 weeks before the employee starts the new job.

Core Rules

Residence Permit TypeEmployer Change Rule
HSM (Kennismigrant)New employer must be an IND Recognised Sponsor. New employer must apply for a new HSM residence permit. IND must be notified within 4 weeks. Salary must meet the 2026 threshold.
EU Blue CardNew employer must be an IND Recognised Sponsor. During the first 12 months, changing employer requires a new application; after that, notification is sufficient.
GVVA / TWVEmployer-bound. New employer must repeat UWV labour-market test + IND reissuance.
ICT intra-company transferICT is tied to the specific sending company. During ICT status, the employee cannot transfer into a local Dutch company and may only move among group companies in the EU under ICT Mobile.
Orientation Year (Zoekjaar)12 months of free employment. After finding HSM employment, may transfer to HSM. Not employer-bound.
Permanent residence (Onbepaalde Tijd)Fully free, no employer restriction.

Practical Steps to Change Employers

Employee signs an employment contract with the new employer; confirm the new employer is an IND Recognised Sponsor and the salary meets the 2026 threshold.
New employer submits the HSM change of employer application through IND Business Portal, uploading the new contract + salary proof + bank account proof.
IND decides within 2–4 weeks. During this period, the employee may continue working for the original employer or remain unemployed, as long as they remain within valid legal status.
Original employer must report “salary stopped / employment ended” through IND Business Portal within 4 weeks after the employee’s actual departure.
HSM unemployment grace period: the employee may remain in the Netherlands for up to 3 months to seek a new job without losing legal residence. If no new sponsor is found after 3 months, the employee must leave or switch to another visa.
Collect the new residence card if the validity period needs to change; IND sends it to the registered address.

Important Reminder
The HSM unemployment grace period is 3 months, counted from the date IND receives the employer’s “salary stopped” report. If no new sponsor is found during this period, the residence permit may be revoked and the employee must leave within 28 days after the deadline. It is recommended to secure the new HSM application before the original employment ends to avoid a status gap. From 1 January 2026, IND strengthens salary payment compliance: actual payment evidence into the employee’s own bank account must be provided.

Permanent Residence and Employer Change

After 5 consecutive years of legal residence as an HSM / Blue Card holder, including unemployment grace periods not exceeding 6 months in total, and after passing the integration exam (Inburgeringsexamen) A2 or B1 in some cases, the employee may apply for permanent residence (Verblijfsvergunning Onbepaalde Tijd).
Permanent residence removes employer binding completely and allows free employment / self-employment / entrepreneurship.

7. Departure Guide

Submit resignation notice according to the employment contract (opzegtermijn), usually 1–4 months depending on seniority. Employer completes final salary settlement + holiday allowance (vakantiegeld 8%).
If transitievergoeding applies (such as contract expiry or economic dismissal), settle transition compensation: 1/3 monthly salary per year of service, 2026 cap €98,000.
Employer reports employee departure (“employment ended”) through IND Business Portal within 4 weeks. The residence permit may become invalid afterwards.
Deregister from BRP at the local municipality (gemeente) (uitschrijving uit BRP), using departure ticket or new address proof. Earliest can be 5 days before departure.
Belastingdienst tax filing: in the departure year, submit the “M-form” (migratie-aangifte) to report worldwide income; excess withheld tax may be refunded.
Pension (AOW): accrues by year and remains in the system after departure. Full AOW requires 50 years of residence. Non-EU employees may consider transfer into the home-country system where a bilateral agreement exists, such as the limited China-Netherlands social security agreement.
Close Dutch bank account: keep it for 1–2 months to settle final iDEAL / direct debit payments. Clear all recurring payments before closure.
Cancel subscriptions: basic health insurance (Zorgverzekering) must be cancelled in writing, with premium refunded pro rata; phone / internet contracts usually require 1 month notice.
Return residence card where requested by some IND service desks.
If there is a possibility of returning to the Netherlands: keep BSN, which is valid for life, and DigiD digital identity account, to support remote tax / refund procedures.

8. Key Policy Changes from 2023 to 2026

In recent years, the Netherlands has been balancing continued attraction of high-skilled talent with tighter tax incentives and stronger compliance. Several new rules have taken effect in 2026.

HSM salary thresholds increased (effective 1 January 2026)
Under 30 years old €4,357, approximately +4.5%; 30 years and over €5,942; Orientation Year €3,122.
IND application fees increased (2026)
Main work-category applications such as HSM / Blue Card / ICT / GVVA are unified at €423. Sponsor registration fee €4,294; companies with fewer than 50 employees pay half.
Sponsor compliance upgraded (effective 1 January 2026)
Employers must keep evidence that actual salary was paid into the employee’s own bank account, such as bank statements or batch payment lists; payslips alone are no longer sufficient.
30% ruling adjustment (2025–2027)
2025 salary cap follows the “Balkenende-norm”; 2026 remains 30%; from 1 January 2027 it uniformly decreases to 27%. Pre-2024 holders have transitional rules.
Box 2/3 partial foreign taxpayer status cancelled (effective 1 January 2025)
30% ruling holders no longer enjoy partial foreign taxpayer exemption for Box 2/3. Existing 2023 beneficiaries have transition until end-2026.
RNI registration rule change (effective 1 January 2026)
Non-EU nationals without a European passport may no longer use temporary RNI registration at The Hague (Den Haag) municipality; they must go to Breda or Venlo. Stays over 4 months go directly through BRP.
EU Blue Card new rules (effective November 2024)
Blue Card threshold reduced to 1.5× the social average wage; IT specialists with 3 years of experience may be exempt from the degree requirement.
Orientation Year coverage expanded
Covers graduates from THE / ARWU / QS global top 200 universities within 3 years of graduation, regardless of nationality. MVV country list adjusted since 2024: some small countries no longer require MVV; China remains an MVV-required country.
China Apostille simplification (November 2023)
China officially joined the Hague Convention. Chinese-issued public documents such as birth / marriage / academic certificates can be used in the Netherlands after Apostille, without double authentication.
