COMPLIANCE

Licensing &
Regulatory Compliance

Global payroll is a regulated industry. Marco holds the licenses required to operate legally in every market it serves — not just certifications, but government-issued authorizations tied to named legal entities.

OUR POSITION

A license is not
a formality.
It is legal standing.

Many payroll providers operate in markets where they are technically required to hold a license but have not obtained one. Marco does not take that approach.

Every license Marco holds is tied to a specific legal entity, a specific jurisdiction, and a specific scope of activity. Obtaining them takes time. That is the point — it is proof that Marco has gone through the regulatory process, not around it.

As Marco expands into new markets, in-market licensing is part of the entry process, not an afterthought.

WHY IT MATTERS

What local licensing
actually delivers

Beyond legal obligation, in-market licenses give Marco the operational authority to deliver services that unlicensed providers cannot.

Financial Licensing

Authorizes Marco to conduct cross-border payment, foreign exchange, and payment service provider activities from licensed entities. Clients transacting through Marco do so within a regulated payment framework.

Tax-Agent Qualifications

Enables Marco to file taxes, lodge statutory returns, and provide payroll tax advisory services on behalf of clients in registered markets. Unregistered providers cannot legally perform these activities.

In-Market Operations

Local licensing is a prerequisite for Marco's owned-entity model. It is what allows Marco to act as a legal employer, process statutory contributions, and engage with local regulators directly on behalf of clients.
